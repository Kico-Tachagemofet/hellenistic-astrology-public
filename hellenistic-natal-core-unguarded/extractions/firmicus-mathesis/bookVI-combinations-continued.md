# Firmicus Extraction - Book VI Combinations Continued

Source: Julius Firmicus Maternus, *Mathesis*, Book VI, translated by Jean
Rhys Bram, *Ancient Astrology Theory and Practice*.

Scope:

- trine, square, opposition, sextile, and conjunction combination catalogs;

## Method Takeaway

Book VI is another high-risk cookbook zone, but it has several strong process
rules beneath the catalogs:

- the quality of the house changes the same planetary mixture;
- aspect family matters, but the planet holding the superior/right-hand
  position often matters more;
- benefics and malefics alter each other through aspect, co-presence, and host
  condition;
- exact degree, terms, sign nature, Moon phase, sect, and angularity are
  required in the severe examples;
- derived topic houses supplement, but do not erase, general house topics;
- chronocrator periods actualize what the natal chart already contains.

No direct sexual, medical, death, enslavement, punishment, or status labels from
Book VI are promoted into reusable delineation.

## Rule Card 6.1 - House Class Precedes Combination Judgment

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.1] Bram printed pp. 182-183.

Use when:

- judging any planetary combination;
- comparing the same aspect or conjunction in different houses.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant, MC, Descendant, and IMC;
- whole-sign house positions;
- house class for each planet;
- planet condition and sect;
- aspects to the Ascendant.

Procedure:

1. Classify the angles before reading the combination: Ascendant and MC are
   first angles; Descendant and IMC are second angles.
2. Distinguish favorable houses from disconnected/dejected houses.
3. Treat benefics in hostile houses as weakened and malefics in hostile houses
   as intensified.
4. Add Moon involvement as a major amplifier when malefics are in the hostile
   houses.

Guardrail:

Do not reuse a planet-pair result without house quality. Firmicus opens Book VI
by making house placement a prerequisite for combination judgment.

## Rule Card 6.2 - Bright-Star Claims Require Exact Degree, Moon Phase, and Angle

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.2] Bram printed pp. 183-184.

Use when:

- a bright fixed-star degree is proposed as a major dignity or eminence factor;
- Moon degree is near one of Firmicus's regal bright-star examples.

Required chart data:

- Moon degree and minute;
- fixed-star degree list, if supported;
- Moon phase;
- angularity;
- Jupiter aspect.

Procedure:

1. Require exact degree data before using the bright-star claim.
2. Require Moon angularity, especially on Ascendant or MC, for strong outcomes.
3. Check whether the Moon is waxing/full and whether Jupiter offers favorable
   testimony.
4. Treat these examples as degree doctrine requiring verification against Book
   VIII and page images.

## Rule Card 6.3 - Trine Softens Malefics and Strengthens Benefics Only When Supported

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.3-VI.8] Bram printed pp. 184-187.

Use when:

- two planets are in sign-based trine;
- a trine is being treated as automatically beneficial.

Required chart data:

- planets in trine;
- house quality of both planets;
- dignity/rejoicing/exaltation;
- aspect to the Ascendant;
- Moon phase and Moon aspect;
- sect and Mars involvement.

Procedure:

1. Check that both planets are in favorable houses before using the trine as a
   strong support.
2. Inspect dignity, rejoicing, or own-house placement for both planets.
3. Add Ascendant or waxing-Moon testimony as an amplifier.
4. Treat Mars involvement, contrary sect, or waning Moon as weakening or
   complicating the promise.
5. Route specific outcomes by planet pair only after the full condition stack
   passes.

Guardrail:

Trine is not enough by itself. In Book VI the trine works best when house,
dignity, sect, Moon, and benefic support all cooperate.

## Rule Card 6.4 - Superior / Right-Hand Square Changes the Outcome

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.9-VI.14] Bram printed pp. 187-193.

Use when:

- two planets are in square aspect;
- deciding which planet dominates the square.

Required chart data:

- both planets in square;
- right/left square relationship;
- which planet holds the superior or upper position;
- house quality and angularity;
- sect;
- benefic/malefic mitigation.

Procedure:

1. Identify the square and determine which planet is in the superior/right-hand
   position.
2. If a malefic holds the superior position over a benefic, mark the contact as
   more obstructive.
3. If a benefic holds the superior position over a malefic, reduce the severity
   and check for useful outcomes.
4. Compare the result with house quality, sect, and other aspects before
   judgment.

Guardrail:

Do not read square as a single undifferentiated malefic contact. Book VI changes
the prediction when the superior planet changes.

## Rule Card 6.5 - Square and Opposition Are Severe, But Mitigation Is Required

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.9-VI.20] Bram printed pp. 187-197.

Use when:

- a square or opposition is used for danger, loss, illness, conflict, or
  reversal claims.

Required chart data:

- planets in square/opposition;
- angularity and house class;
- sign type;
- benefic aspects from Jupiter or Venus;
- malefic aspects from Saturn or Mars;
- Moon phase when Moon is involved.

Procedure:

1. Mark square and opposition as severe aspect families in Firmicus.
2. Check whether the aspect involves angles, the Moon, or the Ascendant.
3. Inspect sign nature only as a secondary modifier.
4. Search for Jupiter/Venus mitigation before allowing a severe claim.
5. If benefic testimony exists, soften, redirect, or condition the claim.

Guardrail:

Most Book VI square/opposition examples contain sensitive topics. Preserve the
mitigation structure, not the literal outcome list.

## Rule Card 6.7 - Conjunction Is Shared Hospitality, Not Automatic Fusion

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.22-VI.27] Bram printed pp. 197-203.

Use when:

- planets are in the same sign or same degree;
- judging co-presence as a single result.

Required chart data:

- planets in the same sign or degree;
- exact degree order;
- house quality;
- dignity/rejoicing;
- sect;
- solar phase;
- benefic/malefic witnesses;
- Moon phase and degree order where relevant.

Procedure:

1. Distinguish same-sign co-presence from same-degree conjunction.
2. Inspect whether the planets share a favorable house, dignity, or rejoicing
   condition.
3. Check whether one planet is hidden by the Sun or otherwise weakened.
4. Use degree order when Firmicus explicitly changes the prediction by which
   planet has more degrees.
5. Add benefic mitigation or malefic exposure before judging the result.

Guardrail:

Do not treat conjunction as inherently good or bad. The same conjunction can
produce very different testimony through sect, phase, house, and witness.

## Rule Card 6.8 - Co-Presence Can Blend or Counteract Planetary Temperaments

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.22] Bram printed pp. 197-198.

Use when:

- two planets with contrary qualities are co-present;
- interpreting Mars/Saturn or similar mixed combinations.

Required chart data:

- co-present planets;
- angularity;
- dignity and house quality;
- benefic mitigation;
- sect.

Procedure:

1. Identify whether the planets intensify each other or counterbalance each
   other.
2. For Mars/Saturn, treat the source's logic as a blending of heat and cold:
   each planet restrains the other's excess but can also impede activity.
3. Increase severity if the pair is angular and unaided by Jupiter.
4. Keep any literal illness or death language historical/deferred.

Guardrail:

A conjunction is not a simple sum of two planet keywords. Firmicus sometimes
uses contrary powers as balancing or canceling mechanisms.

## Rule Card 6.9 - Prenatal Moon Attachment Is a Birth-Threshold Omen

## Rule Card 6.10 - Ill-Omen Charts Are Multi-Factor Threshold Examples

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.29] Bram printed pp. 204-206.

Use when:

- reading severe multi-planet examples;
- deciding whether to preserve a negative chart pattern.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant degree and angular axis;
- Moon phase and angularity;
- Jupiter/Venus protection;
- Saturn/Mars angular aspects;
- malefic enclosure or attack;
- dejected-house placement of benefics.

Procedure:

1. Extract only the structural pattern: malefics attacking Ascendant, Moon, or a
   benefic without benefic mitigation.

   factor.
4. Defer direct claims about enslavement, exposure, death, punishment, illness,

## Rule Card 6.12 - Famous Charts Are Rhetorical Examples, Not Validation Cases

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.30] Bram printed pp. 206-210.

Use when:

- Firmicus attaches famous names to a chart configuration;
- evaluating whether a historical example should become a case.

Required chart data:

- full chart configuration in the source;
- topic being illustrated;
- degree/house/aspect structure.

Procedure:

1. Treat the famous chart as an illustration of Firmicus's method.
2. Extract only reusable structural ideas, such as angular clustering, benefic
   support, malefic attack, or fifth/ninth house support for arts, speech, and
   philosophy.
3. Do not treat the example as proof that the chart belongs to the named
   figure.

Archimedes into regression cases in this extraction pass.

## Rule Card 6.14 - Derived Topic Houses Supplement General House Topics

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.32] Bram printed pp. 219-225.

Use when:

- investigating a specific life topic in Firmicus's method;
- a general house topic is too broad for the judgment.

Required chart data:

- exact planetary degrees;
- Ascendant degree;
- chart sect;
- topic formula pair;
- ruler of the derived topic sign;
- term ruler of the derived topic degree;
- benefic/malefic aspects.

Procedure:

1. Compute the derived topic house using the relevant planet-to-planet arc.
2. Project the degree count from the Ascendant through the signs.
3. Inspect the resulting sign, its ruler, the term ruler, occupants, and
   benefic/malefic aspects.
4. Compare the derived house with the general house topic instead of replacing
   it.

Guardrail:

Firmicus explicitly says ordinary houses alone are not enough for some topics.
Do not judge father, spouse, children, illness, and related matters from the
general house alone.

## Rule Card 6.18 - Chronocrator Periods Actualize Natal Promise

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VI.33-VI.39] Bram printed pp. 226-232.

Use when:

- interpreting a planet as ruler of time;
- judging whether a period produces good or bad results.

Required chart data:

- current chronocrator;
- natal condition of the chronocrator;
- chart sect;
- house/sign/degree placement;
- angularity;
- aspects;
- chart ruler relation;
- subperiod recipient.

Procedure:

1. Judge what the planet promises in the natal chart before reading the period.
2. When it becomes ruler of time, expect it to actualize its natal promise.
3. Modify the result by sect, house quality, sign/degree condition, and aspects.
4. Read subperiods as the chronocrator distributing months to another planet,
   whose nature modifies the period.

Guardrail:

Time-lord rules do not create events from nothing. They activate natal
testimony.
