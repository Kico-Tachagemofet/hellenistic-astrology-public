# Firmicus Extraction - Book V Combinations

Source: Julius Firmicus Maternus, *Mathesis*, Book V, translated by Jean
Rhys Bram, *Ancient Astrology Theory and Practice*.

Scope:

- sign natures on angles;
- ascendant terms and planets occupying those terms;
- Saturn and Jupiter sign catalogs;
- lacuna for Sun, Venus, and Mars sign catalogs;
- Mercury and Moon in terms/decans;
- Book V's concluding whole-chart synthesis warning.

Copyright note: this file paraphrases method and stores page references. It
does not reproduce long source passages.

## Chapter Range

- Bram printed pp. 155-181.
- PDF pp. 159-185.

## Method Takeaway

Book V is a controlled cookbook zone. Its sign-by-sign and term-by-term
material is not extracted as direct delineation text. The useful method is the
conditioning logic behind the catalog:

- sign effects require an angle or another strong carrier;
- the Ascendant is modified by term ruler and planets in the terms;
- planet-in-sign material is repeatedly gated by sect, phase, angularity,
  benefic/malefic testimony, time rulers, and transits;
- the conclusion explicitly forbids single-factor prediction.

Book V's strongest contribution to the project is therefore not a list of
placements. It is a synthesis guardrail: no topic claim should be promoted
unless the whole chart's structure and the relevant time lord support it.

## Rule Card 5.1 - Book V Must Be Read as Synthesis Material

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.Praef.] Bram printed pp. 155-156; [Firmicus
*Math.* V.7] Bram printed pp. 180-181.

Use when:

- extracting from a placement-by-placement passage in Book V;
- deciding whether a catalog judgment can become a reusable rule.

Required chart data:

- full natal chart;
- angularity;
- sign/degree/term/decan data;
- planetary aspects and ruler chains;
- timing/time-lord context when a timed outcome is claimed.

Procedure:

1. Treat Book V placement paragraphs as evidence examples, not as standalone
   delineation rules.
2. Extract only the recurring condition logic: dignity, sect, phase,
   angularity, ruler/host relation, benefic/malefic testimony, and timing.
3. If a paragraph only says "planet in sign produces outcome" without
   extractable method, leave it as deferred cookbook material.
4. Preserve the conclusion's rule: a planet's place, a house topic, or a
   transit is not sufficient unless the chart's whole essence supports it.

Guardrail:

Do not build modern output from the Book V catalogs by placement alone.

## Rule Card 5.2 - Sign Nature Requires an Angular Carrier

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.1] Bram printed pp. 156-163.

Use when:

- evaluating whether a bare sign nature should affect delineation;
- deciding whether Book V's sign catalog can be reused.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant sign;
- MC, Descendant, and IMC signs;
- sign occupying each angle.

Procedure:

1. Record the four angle signs.
2. Apply sign-nature testimony only where the sign is carried by an angle or
   another strong chart point.
3. Treat the Book V angle-sign rotation as source testimony for angular
   activation, not as a universal sign cookbook.

Guardrail:

Do not infer a native's character or fate from a sign's nature unless the sign
is made operative by an angle, planet, Lot, ruler chain, or other relevant
carrier.

## Rule Card 5.4 - Ascendant Terms Modify Body and Life Expression

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.2] Bram printed pp. 163-166.

Use when:

- auditing the Ascendant beyond sign ruler and house placement;
- evaluating bodily, temperament, and life-direction testimony.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant degree;
- bound/term ruler of the Ascendant degree;
- sect;
- Ascendant sign ruler;
- aspects to the Ascendant and its term ruler.

Procedure:

1. Identify the terms/bounds containing the Ascendant degree.
2. Inspect the term ruler's condition: dignity, sect fit, angularity, solar
   phase, and benefic/malefic testimony.
3. Use the term ruler as a modifying layer on bodily and life-expression
   topics.
4. Combine this with the Ascendant sign ruler rather than replacing the sign
   ruler automatically.

Guardrail:

Do not preserve Firmicus's sensitive physiognomic or status labels as
client-facing outcomes. Translate the method into condition strength and topic
routing only.

## Rule Card 5.5 - Planets in Ascendant Terms Condition the Ascendant Testimony

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.2] Bram printed pp. 163-166.

Use when:

- a planet occupies the same terms/bounds as the Ascendant degree;
- the Ascendant term ruler alone does not explain the testimony.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant degree and terms;
- planets occupying those terms;
- planetary sect, phase, dignity, angularity, and aspects;
- Moon phase when Moon is involved.

Procedure:

1. Identify planets placed in the Ascendant's terms.
2. Judge each planet's condition before using it as Ascendant testimony.
3. Distinguish benefic/malefic expression by sect and by waxing/waning lunar
   condition where relevant.
4. Combine planet-in-terms testimony with the Ascendant ruler and term ruler.

Guardrail:

A planet in the Ascendant's terms is not a standalone character verdict. It is
an additional conditioned witness.

## Rule Card 5.6 - Saturn Sign Catalog Distills to Delay, Cycle, and Mitigation Logic

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.3] Bram printed pp. 166-174.

Use when:

- reading Firmicus's Saturn-in-sign catalog;
- evaluating Saturn claims about delay, loss, inheritance, marriage, children,
  illness, danger, or status.

Required chart data:

- Saturn sign, house, degree, terms, and dignity;
- Saturn sect condition;
- aspects from Jupiter, Venus, Sun, Moon, and Mars;
- time-lord sequence and transits when a timed crisis is claimed;
- Saturn's first orbit / approximately thirty-year cycle when relevant.

Procedure:

1. Extract whether Saturn is supported by domicile, angle, term/decan, or
   benefic testimony.
2. Check whether Firmicus ties the outcome to Saturn's first orbit or to
   seventh/ninth-year crisis rhythms.
3. Treat Jupiter/Venus support and benefic time rulers as mitigation, delay,
   or reversal factors.
4. Treat malefic testimony, contrary sect, or angular activation as intensifiers
   only after the full chart supports the topic.

Guardrail:

Do not reuse the sign-by-sign Saturn outcomes directly. Most of the catalog is
topic-sensitive and contains death, illness, family, and status predictions that
must remain historical/deferred.

## Rule Card 5.7 - Saturn Delays Do Not Equal Final Denial

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.3] Bram printed pp. 166-174.

Use when:

- Saturn appears in a topic route for marriage, children, inheritance, property,
  office, or livelihood;
- interpreting a Saturn-heavy placement paragraph.

Required chart data:

- topic house and ruler;
- Saturn's relation to the topic;
- benefic witnesses;
- time-lord periods and transits;
- relevant angular houses.

Procedure:

1. Separate Saturn's role into delay, burden, loss, discipline, separation, or
   later consolidation.
2. Check whether the same passage includes relief through benefic aspect, later
   transit, or time-ruler change.
3. Phrase any reusable method as temporal/conditional modulation rather than
   permanent denial.

Guardrail:

Do not say Saturn automatically destroys a topic. Firmicus repeatedly routes
Saturn topics through timing and mitigation.

## Rule Card 5.8 - Jupiter Sign Catalog Requires Timing and Host Conditions

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.4] Bram printed pp. 174-177.

Use when:

- reading Firmicus's Jupiter-in-sign catalog;
- evaluating wealth, status, office, public favor, or later-life improvement
  claims.

Required chart data:

- Jupiter sign, house, dignity, terms/decan, and angularity;
- Jupiter aspects, especially trines from relevant houses;
- Saturn's relation by aspect or transit;
- time-lord periods.

Procedure:

1. Check Jupiter's host sign and house before treating Jupiter as simply
   benefic.
2. Distinguish early difficulty from later rise when the paragraph routes
   outcomes through Saturn periods/transits or later activation.
3. Treat trines, benefic aspects, and angular support as promotion factors.
4. Treat Saturn pressure as a timing and obstruction condition, not as a
   universal cancellation.

Guardrail:

Jupiter in a sign does not automatically promise the topic attached to that
sign or house. The source repeatedly requires chart computation and aspect
combination.

## Rule Card 5.10 - Mercury Terms/Decans Route Speech, Trade, and Reliability Through the Host

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.5] Bram printed p. 178.

Use when:

- Mercury occupies terms or decans of Mars, Sun, Venus, or Moon;
- reading Mercury occupation, speech, trade, writing, or reliability topics.

Required chart data:

- Mercury sign, degree, terms, and decan;
- host planet of Mercury's terms/decan;
- reciprocal term/decan involvement between Mercury and the host;
- Mars aspects when Mars is involved;
- chart sect and solar phase.

Procedure:

1. Identify Mercury's term and decan ruler.
2. Judge the host planet's condition before judging Mercury's expression.
3. If Mercury and Mars occupy each other's terms/decans or Mars squares
   Mercury, mark the combination as a risk intensifier.
4. Distinguish Mercury in Sun terms/decans by day/night condition.
5. Treat Mercury in Venus terms/decans as a host-routed support for arts,
   language, trade, and learning, while checking surrounding scandal/risk
   testimony.

Guardrail:

Do not turn the Mercury paragraph into a moral label. Extract host-routing and
condition logic only.

## Rule Card 5.11 - Moon Terms/Decans Require Phase, Sect, and Host

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.6] Bram printed pp. 178-180.

Use when:

- Moon occupies terms or decans of Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury,
  or her own domicile/decan;
- evaluating bodily condition, fortune, public duties, relationships, travel,
  or livelihood through the Moon.

Required chart data:

- Moon sign, degree, phase, terms, and decan;
- chart sect;
- Moon angularity;
- host planet of the Moon's terms/decan;
- Jupiter and Mars aspects where present.

Procedure:

1. Identify the Moon's phase first, especially full versus waning.
2. Identify the term/decan host and judge that host's condition.
3. Check chart sect because Firmicus changes expression by day/night in several
   Moon cases.
4. Use angularity and Jupiter testimony as elevation/mitigation factors.
5. Use Mars contact as an intensifier only after checking sect, phase, and
   benefic support.

Guardrail:

Moon-in-terms/decan outcomes are not portable placement rules. They are host-
and phase-conditioned lunar testimony.

## Rule Card 5.12 - Whole-Essence Gate for Topic Predictions

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.7] Bram printed pp. 180-181.

Use when:

- predicting marriage from the seventh house;
- predicting children from the fifth house;
- predicting danger from Mars/Saturn transits;
- turning any isolated natal factor into a topic claim.

Required chart data:

- relevant topic house and ruler;
- planets in or aspecting the topic house;
- Ascendant and its witnesses;
- sign and degree quality;
- time-lord and transit context for timed claims;
- benefic/malefic mitigation or intensification.

Procedure:

1. Start from the full chart, not the isolated house or planet.
2. Require topic-house evidence and ruler evidence before making a topic claim.
3. Require time-lord or transit support before making a timed claim.
4. If the full chart contradicts the isolated placement, demote or withhold the
   prediction.

Guardrail:

Firmicus explicitly denies that Jupiter in the seventh always means marriage,
Jupiter in the fifth always means children, or Mars/Saturn transits always mean
danger. Use this as a hard anti-cookbook rule.

## Rule Card 5.13 - Hospitality Changes Benefic and Malefic Expression

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* V.7] Bram printed p. 181.

Use when:

- a benefic occupies a sign, term, decan, or house context hostile to its
  nature;
- a malefic occupies a supportive sign, term, decan, or house context;
- deciding whether planet nature alone is enough.

Required chart data:

- planet's sign, house, degree, terms, and decan;
- dignity/debility;
- host/ruler condition;
- aspects from benefics and malefics;
- sect and angularity.

Procedure:

1. Judge the planet's intrinsic nature.
2. Judge the condition of the place and host receiving it.
3. If a benefic is received by a hostile or weakened place, reduce or complicate
   its promise.
4. If a malefic is received by a supportive or dignified place, reduce or
   redirect its harm.
5. Combine this with sect and aspects before output.

Guardrail:

Do not treat benefic/malefic nature as fixed output. Firmicus gives place and
host condition the power to alter expression.
