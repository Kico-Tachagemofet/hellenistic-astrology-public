# Firmicus Extraction - Book VII Life Prediction

Source: Julius Firmicus Maternus, *Mathesis*, Book VII, translated by Jean
Rhys Bram, *Ancient Astrology Theory and Practice*.

Scope:

- astrologer's oath and restricted teaching frame;
- exposed infants / nurture and survival material;
- twins and multiple births;
- historical slavery/status and captivity topics;
- animals and monstrous births as non-core material;
- physical infirmities, illness, death, legal punishment, and other sensitive
  life topics;
- parents, marriage, children, sexual desire, and occupations.

Copyright note: this file paraphrases method and stores page references. It
does not reproduce long source passages.

## Chapter Range

- Bram printed pp. 233-265.
- PDF pp. 237-269.

## Method Takeaway

Book VII is the most sensitive extraction zone so far. Nearly every technical
chapter contains topics that should not become direct client-facing prediction:
infant death, enslavement, sexual stigma, illness, disability, family violence,
violent death, criminal punishment, and moralized social categories.

The reusable method is the evidence chain:

- identify the topic significator or derived topic house;
- check luminary, Ascendant, Fortune, and ruler condition;
- check malefic attack versus benefic protection;
- check angularity, anaforas/epicataforas, sign type, exact degree, terms, and
  Moon phase;
- treat timing claims as valid only when the relevant time lord supports them.

All direct severe outcomes remain historical/deferred.

## Rule Card 7.2 - Nurture / Exposed-Infant Material Is a Sensitive Threshold Pattern

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VII.2] Bram printed pp. 234-237.

Use when:

- reading the long exposed-infant catalog;
- evaluating any life-survival or early-life severe claim.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant and its ruler;
- Sun, Moon, and their angularity/aspects;
- Part of Fortune and ruler;
- malefics on angles, anaforas, epicataforas, or descendant;

3. Track dejected placements of rulers of Ascendant, Fortune, or luminaries as
   added weakness.
4. Keep the entire chapter historical/deferred for modern output.

Do not predict infant death, exposure, or parental harm from this material. It
is retained only as source evidence for how Firmicus builds severe-threshold

## Rule Card 7.8 - Marriage Topics Use Derived Marriage House plus Venus/Mars Condition

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VII.12-VII.18] Bram printed pp. 247-251.

Use when:

- judging marriage number, spouse type, or timing in Firmicus;
- comparing marriage formulas from Book VI and Book VII.

Required chart data:

- derived house of wife/husband from Book VI.32;
- Venus and Mars condition;
- Saturn/Venus degree order and relation;
- ruler of the derived marriage house;
- sign type of Venus and marriage house;
- Jupiter protection;
- total planetary degree sum for the timing method, if used.

Procedure:

1. Compute the derived marriage house before using the catalog.
2. Inspect Venus in all charts and Mars in a woman's-chart route as general
   marriage significators.
3. Use Saturn/Venus degree order or relation only as a secondary indicator.
4. For marriage timing, inspect the ruler of the marriage house, occupants, and
   Firmicus's alternate total-degree method.
5. Keep incest, spousal murder, sexual violence, and stigmatizing sexual labels
   historical/deferred.

Guardrail:

Do not make direct claims about number of spouses, incest, murder, or sexual
conduct. Use the material only for topic-routing structure.

## Rule Card 7.14 - Occupations Require Fortune, Occupation House, Rulers, and Planetary Skill Significators

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VII.26] Bram printed pp. 264-265.

Use when:

- judging occupation or skill in Firmicus;
- comparing with Book IV occupation significators.

Required chart data:

- Mercury and Sun relation;
- Mars/Jupiter/Mercury/Venus term exchanges;
- Part of Fortune;
- house of occupations;
- ruler of Fortune sign;
- ruler of occupation sign;
- aspects to Ascendant and angles;
- Mercury sign ruler condition.

Procedure:

1. Inspect Part of Fortune and the house of occupations before choosing a skill
   significator.
2. Compare the ruler of Fortune's sign with the ruler of the occupation house.
3. If both rulers are present, choose the better located one as the stronger
   occupational route.
4. Use planet-significator lists only after the ruler and house audit.
5. Check Mercury's sign ruler to decide whether the skill persists profitably or
   is abandoned.

Guardrail:

Do not output a job from a planet keyword alone. Firmicus routes occupation
through Fortune, occupation house, rulers, terms, aspects, and Mercury's ruler.
