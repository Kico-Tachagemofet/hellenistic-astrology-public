# Firmicus Extraction - Book IV Aspects, Lots, Moon Motion, and Timing

Source: Julius Firmicus Maternus, *Mathesis*, Book IV, translated by Jean
Rhys Bram, *Ancient Astrology Theory and Practice*.

Scope:

- Moon phases, applications, separations, conjunctions, and defluctions;
- void-of-course Moon;
- Part of Fortune and Part of Daemon;
- ruler of the chart / `oecodespotes`;
- climacteric years;
- occupations;
- full/empty degrees and masculine/feminine degrees;
- Moon co-presence on angles.

Copyright note: this file paraphrases method and stores page references. It
does not reproduce long source passages.

## Chapter Range

- Bram printed pp. 117-154.
- PDF pp. 121-158.

## Method Takeaway

Book IV turns the Moon into a dynamic audit procedure. Firmicus asks not only
"where is the Moon?" but also: what phase is she in, what planet does she move
toward, from what planet does she separate, what happens on the third day, what
terms/sign boundary is she crossing, and which benefic/malefic witnesses modify
the contact.

Book IV also gives two major routing points for the whole chart:

- Part of Fortune: quality of life, fortune, inheritance, desire, childcare, and
  homeland topics through a ruler stack.
- Ruler of the chart: the planet that controls the total efficacy of the chart,
  chosen by a Moon-based method in Firmicus's preferred approach.

## Rule Card 4.1 - Moon Must Be Audited Alongside Ascendant and MC

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.1] Bram printed pp. 118-119.

Use when:

- building the chart's life/body audit;
- deciding whether Ascendant/MC alone are enough for life-force claims.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant and MC;
- Moon sign, degree, phase, and house;
- Moon aspects/applications/separations.

Procedure:

1. Inspect Ascendant and MC for life/spirit indicators.
2. Inspect the Moon with equal care for the bodily/material vessel.
3. Record Moon degree and course before judging bodily condition, vitality,
   life-flow, or timing.

Guardrail:

Do not treat Ascendant and MC as sufficient for life/body topics in Firmicus.
The Moon is required evidence.

## Rule Card 4.3 - Moon Motion Data Checklist

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.1] Bram printed p. 119.

Use when:

- auditing the Moon before delineation;
- extracting Book IV lunar rules.

Required chart data:

- Moon phase;
- planet she separates from;
- planet she applies/moves toward;
- second aspect after first contact;
- whether she is high/low in course if the source technique uses it;
- aspect type: conjunction, trine, square, opposition;
- chart sect.

Procedure:

1. Identify the Moon's phase: synodic, full, half, new, gibbous, waxing, or
   waning as available.
2. Identify the planet she moves away from.
3. Identify the planet she moves toward.
4. Identify her next/second association after the first.
5. Record aspect type and whether the contact is with benefic or malefic.
6. Interpret lunar contact only after phase, direction, and testimony are known.

Guardrail:

Moon aspect alone is not enough. Phase and direction are required fields for
Firmicus.

## Rule Card 4.6 - Moon Moving Toward Mars

## Rule Card 4.8 - Void-of-Course Moon

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.8] Bram printed p. 123.

Use when:

- Moon is moving toward no planet / making no next aspect;
- assessing early obstruction or lack of connection.

Required chart data:

- Moon next aspect;
- benefic planets on angles;
- malefic planets on angles;
- first-day and third-day Moon condition if available.

Procedure:

1. Mark Moon as void when she is moving toward nothing and aspects no planet.
2. If no benefic planet is on an angle, Firmicus treats the condition as severe
   deprivation/obstruction testimony.
3. Benefic aspect or angular support does not erase the obstruction but can
   allow later recovery after early difficulty.
4. Malefic contact to a void Moon or malefics on angles intensify the condition.

Guardrail:

Do not use modern void-of-course electional rules automatically. This is a
Firmicus natal rule and should be tagged as such.

## Rule Card 4.10 - Moon Separating Toward Nothing

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.15] Bram printed pp. 133-134.

Use when:

- Moon has separated from a planet and applies to no next planet.

Required chart data:

- separating planet;
- Moon phase;
- sign/house quality;
- benefic/malefic testimony.

Procedure:

1. Identify the planet from which the Moon has just separated.
2. If the Moon then moves toward nothing, treat the result as a failure,
   delay, depletion, or inability to complete the prior planet's promise.
3. Interpret according to the separating planet:
   - from Saturn: inheritance/family/bodily burden;
   - from Jupiter: loss of prior fortune or slavery/captivity imagery in the
     source;
   - from Mars: danger, bodily harm, violent labor imagery;
   - from Sun: poverty/wandering/later mitigation;
   - from Venus: relational/scandal/poverty imagery;
   - from Mercury: learning/finance possible after delay, or illness/confusion
     if waning.

## Rule Card 4.12 - Part of Fortune Formula

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.17] Bram printed pp. 135-136.

- calculating Firmicus's Part of Fortune;

Required chart data:

- Sun degree;

1. Firmicus gives a sign-count summary:
   - nocturnal: count signs from Moon to Sun, then from Ascendant;

   - nocturnal: count degrees from Moon to Sun, then project from Ascendant.

## Rule Card 4.13 - Part of Fortune Ruler Stack

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.17] Bram printed pp. 136-137.

Use when:

- auditing Fortune after calculation;
- judging whether Fortune supports or hinders the chart.

Required chart data:

- Fortune sign, degree, house, and terms;
- ruler of Fortune's sign;
- ruler of Fortune's degree/terms;
- ruler of the sect luminary's degree/terms;
- aspects among these rulers and Fortune;
- angle exactness;
- Fortune duodecatemorion.

Procedure:

1. Treat Fortune as measuring quality of life, inheritance, good/bad fortune,
   love/desire, childcare effects, and homeland/fatherland topics.
2. Inspect ruler of Fortune's sign and ruler of Fortune's terms.
3. Inspect the ruler of the Moon's degree in nocturnal charts or Sun's degree in
   diurnal charts.
4. If one planet rules multiple relevant degrees and is favorably placed, mark a
   strong Fortune testimony.
5. If multiple rulers exist, prioritize the one with greatest power.
6. Exact angle placement is stronger than rough/angular-by-sign placement.
7. If the key rulers are badly placed, check whether benefics on MC or the
   Ascendant's anafora mitigate over time.
8. Inspect Fortune's duodecatemorion.

Guardrail:

Fortune is not judged by sign placement alone. It requires a ruler stack and
aspect audit.

## Rule Card 4.15 - Ruler of the Chart Selection

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.19] Bram printed p. 138.

Use when:

- comparing oecodespotes / chart ruler methods;
- selecting Firmicus's preferred ruler of chart.

Required chart data:

- Moon sign at birth;
- sign sequence after the Moon;
- domicile rulers;
- exclusion rule for Sun and Moon as chart ruler.

Procedure:

1. Firmicus reports multiple prior methods:
   - planet in favorable house in its own sign/terms;
   - ruler of the sect luminary's terms;
   - ruler of the Moon's exaltation;
   - ruler of the sign the Moon enters after her natal sign.
2. Firmicus chooses the last method.
3. Find the sign the Moon next enters after her birth sign.
4. The domicile ruler of that sign becomes ruler of the chart.
5. If the Moon next enters Cancer or Leo, skip those signs because Sun and Moon
   do not accept individual chart rulership in Firmicus; continue to the next
   non-luminary domicile.

## Rule Card 4.16 - Ruler of the Chart Audit

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.19] Bram printed pp. 138-144.

Use when:

- auditing Firmicus's oecodespotes after selection;
- judging whole-chart efficacy.

Required chart data:

- selected ruler of chart;
- ruler house, sign, terms, exaltation/fall;
- chart sect and whether it matches the ruler's condition;
- benefic/malefic aspects;
- Sun/Moon co-presence/aspects;
- house/sign nature.

Procedure:

1. Treat the ruler of the chart as controlling the total chart and the efficacy
   of individual planets.
2. Mark strong condition if it is in signs where it rejoices, exaltation, own
   house, own terms, chart condition, and protected by benefics.
3. Mark weakened condition if attacked by malefics or abandoned by benefic
   protection.
4. Judge each planetary ruler according to its nature, but only after house,
   sign, sect, and testimony are checked.
5. Do not isolate the ruler. Firmicus insists that the ruler's effects come from
   mixtures with other planets, houses, signs, and conditions.

Guardrail:

The ruler of chart is a synthesis node, not a single magic key. It gathers and
modifies the chart; it does not replace the Evidence Ledger.

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.20] Bram printed pp. 144-145.

Use when:

- cataloging Firmicus timing doctrine;
- deciding whether to operationalize life-period crises.

Required chart data:

- lifespan/timing framework;
- ruler of chart;
- ruler of time;
- Moon;
- benefic aspects to Moon/rulers;
- planet causing the danger and its house/sign/degree.

Procedure:

1. Track seven-year and nine-year periods and their multiples.

## Rule Card 4.20 - Occupation Significators

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.21] Bram printed pp. 145-147.

Use when:

- determining profession/occupation in Firmicus;
- extracting occupation logic without copying job lists.

Required chart data:

- Mars, Venus, Mercury placements;
- MC and trines to MC;
- angles;
- planet dignity, terms, exaltation;
- chart sect;
- benefic/malefic aspects;
- sign type.

Procedure:

1. Occupations are determined primarily by Mars, Venus, and Mercury.
2. The determining planet is the one on the MC, in right/left trine to MC, or on
   another angle.
3. Mars points to arms, fire/iron, command, danger, and strenuous skills,
   especially when nocturnal and benefic-supported.
4. Venus points to honors, women, art, music, luxury crafts, performance,
   pleasure industries, and refined/manual arts, depending on dignity and house.
5. Mercury points to writing, speech, calculation, medicine, astrology,
   administration, trade, translation, money-changing, and technical knowledge.
6. Benefic testimony increases fame, nobility, and opportunity; malefic testimony
   lowers status, reputation, or stability.
7. Sign type and sign quality select the concrete occupational field.

Guardrail:

Do not output a job title from one planet. Profession is planet + MC relation +
condition + testimony + sign quality.

## Rule Card 4.24 - Moon Conjunction and Defluction by Terms and Boundaries

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* IV.25] Bram printed pp. 153-154.

Use when:

- applying detailed lunar conjunction/defluction doctrine;
- checking Moon at early or late degrees of a sign.

Required chart data:

- Moon sign, exact degree, and terms;
- term ruler of Moon's position;
- whether term ruler aspects the Moon and from what house;
- the planet whose sign the Moon enters in the third place;
- Moon's proximity to sign boundary.

Procedure:

1. Identify the sign/terms from which the Moon is receding.
2. Check whether the ruler of the terms aspects her and from what house.
3. Inspect the planet whose sign the Moon will enter in the third place; that
   planet also has predictive force.
4. If the Moon is in the last degrees of a sign, Firmicus says she does not
   indicate normally because she is impeded by the next sign.
5. If the Moon is in the first degrees and receding from no one, judge only the
   effects of the aspects.
6. Combine all data: planets, houses, signs, sign nature, house quality, planet
   conditions, courses, phases, conjunctions, and defluctions.

Guardrail:

This is a strong evidence-chain rule. Firmicus closes Book IV by requiring the
whole body of data before a true forecast.
