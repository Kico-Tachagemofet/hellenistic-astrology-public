# Firmicus Extraction - Book VIII Decans, Sphaera Barbarica, and Degree Catalogs

Source: Julius Firmicus Maternus, *Mathesis*, Book VIII, translated by Jean
Rhys Bram, *Ancient Astrology Theory and Practice*.

Scope:

- astrologer's creed and conclusion;
- enenecontameris / 90th degree;
- signs that see and hear each other;
- body-zodiac degree distribution;
- Sphaera Barbarica adjacent constellations;
- Myriogenesis-like degree catalog by Ascendant degree;
- bright-star degree summaries;
- final whole-data requirement.

Copyright note: this file paraphrases method and stores page references. It
does not reproduce long source passages.

## Chapter Range

- Bram printed pp. 266-302.
- PDF pp. 270-306.

## Method Takeaway

Book VIII is mostly exact-degree catalog material. Its reusable value is
procedural:

- exact degree measurement is mandatory;
- degree doctrines are secondary overlays, not replacements for ordinary chart
  judgment;
- bright stars, Sphaera Barbarica constellations, and degree-body zones require
  specific rising/setting or Moon/Ascendant contact;
- malefic and benefic aspects alter the degree testimony;
- the book closes by saying that Sphaera Barbarica cannot explain a chart by
  itself.

The degree-by-degree and constellation catalogs are preserved as deferred
source inventories, not as modern delineation recipes.

## Rule Card 8.1 - Creed Becomes Equanimity and Restricted-Use Context

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.1] Bram printed pp. 266-267; [Firmicus
*Math.* VIII.33] Bram printed p. 302.

Use when:

- deciding how to frame Book VIII material;
- handling the source's religious and restricted-circulation claims.

Required chart data:

- none; practitioner context.

Procedure:

1. Treat the creed as historical/religious context rather than technical rule.
2. Translate its practical function into output restraint and emotional
   equanimity.
3. Keep restricted material away from sensational client-facing claims.

Guardrail:

Do not import Firmicus's religious polemic as project doctrine. Preserve only
its process value: caution, seriousness, and restraint.

## Rule Card 8.2 - The Ninetieth Degree Is a Derived Degree from Ascendant and Moon

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.2] Bram printed pp. 267-268.

Use when:

- investigating Firmicus's enenecontameris doctrine;
- comparing exact-degree derived points.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant degree;
- Moon degree;
- sign and terms of each 90th-degree point;
- ruler of each point's sign;
- ruler condition;
- benefic/malefic aspects to each point;
- Moon phase.

Procedure:

1. Count 90 degrees from the Ascendant.
2. Audit the resulting sign, sign ruler, ruler terms, house, and aspects.
3. Count 90 degrees from the Moon and repeat the audit.
4. Judge benefic/malefic contacts as modifiers of the point.
5. Use Moon waxing/waning when Mars or Saturn aspects the Moon-derived 90th
   degree.

Guardrail:

Do not let the 90th degree replace the whole chart. Firmicus says it can reveal
the chart's essence, but Book VIII later requires all data to be combined.

## Rule Card 8.3 - Signs That See and Hear Are a Secondary Relationship Doctrine

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.3] Bram printed pp. 268-269.

- deciding whether to add Sphaera Barbarica sign testimony.

Required chart data:

- sign placements;
- ordinary sign-based aspects;
- hearing/seeing table from Book VIII.3;
- topic being judged.

Procedure:

1. Check ordinary aspects and aversion first.
2. Use seeing/hearing only as a secondary testimony if Phase B tables it.
3. Preserve asymmetry: some signs hear without seeing, see without hearing, or

## Rule Card 8.4 - Individual Degrees Map to Sign Bodies for Body/Affliction Work

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.4] Bram printed pp. 269-271.

Required chart data:

- exact degree within sign;
- planet or point occupying that degree;
- sign-body degree table;

## Rule Card 8.5 - Sphaera Barbarica Uses Adjacent Constellation Risings/Settings

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.5-VIII.17] Bram printed pp. 271-281.

Use when:

- evaluating extra-zodiacal constellation material near the zodiac;
- deciding whether a star/constellation contact is operative.

Required chart data:

- exact Ascendant/Descendant/MC degree;
- adjacent constellation rising/setting degree;
- planet aspects to that place;
- benefic/malefic testimony;
- Moon and Mars/Saturn involvement where specified.

Procedure:

1. Identify whether the relevant constellation is rising, setting, or on the
   MC/angle.
2. Treat rising as an occupational/character/skill indicator in the source.
3. Treat setting plus malefic aspect as a danger/death indicator in the source.
4. Treat benefic aspect as mitigation, protection, or conversion toward skill
   and livelihood.
5. Defer direct catalog use until a table exists.

Guardrail:

The Sphaera Barbarica catalog is not a general natal reading engine. Use only
with exact degree and verified constellation mapping.

## Rule Card 8.6 - Rising/Setting Star Logic Is Aspect-Conditioned

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.6-VIII.17] Bram printed pp. 272-281.

Use when:

- a constellation degree is on the Ascendant, Descendant, or MC;
- deciding whether a star produces skill, danger, or status.

Required chart data:

- exact degree of the angle;
- star/constellation matched to that degree;
- Mars/Saturn/Jupiter/Venus/Mercury aspects;
- Moon condition where specified.

Procedure:

1. Start from the star's rising/setting condition.
2. Add planetary aspects as the decisive modifier.
3. Treat Mars/Saturn contacts as intensifiers or danger modifiers in the
   source.
4. Treat Jupiter/Venus contacts as mitigation or elevation.
5. Treat Mercury/Venus/Mars contacts as occupation-routing modifiers where the
   catalog specifies arts, athletics, ships, weapons, animals, or craft.

Guardrail:

Do not output a literal occupation or death mode from a star name. Use the
pattern only as exact-degree testimony.

## Rule Card 8.7 - Myriogenesis-Like Degree Catalog Requires Exact Birth Degree

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.18] Bram printed p. 281.

Use when:

- handling the degree-by-degree Ascendant catalog;
- deciding whether approximate birth data can support Book VIII claims.

Required chart data:

- exact Ascendant degree and minute;
- verified zodiacal degree table;
- planet aspects to the degree.

Procedure:

1. Require exact Ascendant degree before using any degree catalog.
2. Reject approximate-birth use of degree-by-degree rules.
3. Treat the catalog as analogous to Myriogenesis but at the degree level.
4. Defer minute-level Myriogenesis material.

Guardrail:

Firmicus says incorrect measurement makes the forecast untrustworthy. Do not
use Book VIII degree catalogs without exact birth data.

## Rule Card 8.9 - Bright Stars Require Degree Contact plus Moon/Jupiter Support

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.31] Bram printed pp. 300-301.

Use when:

- assessing a bright-star eminence claim;
- comparing Book VI bright-star examples with Book VIII.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant or Moon on the bright-star degree;
- Jupiter in the degree or trine/aspect where specified;
- Moon condition;
- Mars/Saturn contact where specified;
- chart topic under judgment.

Procedure:

1. Confirm the exact bright-star degree.
2. Check whether the Ascendant, Moon, or named planet occupies the degree.
3. Require Jupiter support or well-located Moon where the source specifies it.
4. Treat Mars/Saturn approaches as crisis modifiers, not as automatic reversal.
5. Cross-check against Book VI before any Phase B use.

Guardrail:

Do not treat bright-star degrees as unconditional royal indicators. They require
exact degree and supporting testimony.

## Rule Card 8.10 - Whole Data Requirement Overrides Sphaera Barbarica

Source: [Firmicus *Math.* VIII.32] Bram printed pp. 301-302.

Use when:

- applying Sphaera Barbarica, bright-star, 90th-degree, or degree-body
  doctrines;
- deciding whether exact-degree testimony is sufficient.

Required chart data:

- houses;
- planets;
- planetary combinations and aspects;
- dignity/debility;
- exaltation, terms, and domicile/house;
- all degree overlays being considered.

Procedure:

1. Collect the Sphaera Barbarica or degree testimony.
2. Compare it with all ordinary houses and planetary conditions.
3. Reduce a planet's testimony if it is in a dejected house or debility.
4. Increase a planet's testimony if it has authority from house, exaltation,
   terms, or domicile.
5. Judge only after inspecting the whole body of the chart.

Guardrail:

Firmicus explicitly says Sphaera Barbarica cannot explain the entire chart by
itself. This is a hard anti-cookbook rule.
