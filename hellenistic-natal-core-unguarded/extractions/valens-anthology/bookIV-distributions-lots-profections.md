# Valens Extraction - Book IV Distributions, Lots, And Annual Frames

Source: Vettius Valens, `Anthologies`, Book IV, Mark Riley translation.

Range: local PDF pp. 71-94.

Brennan cross-ref: `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.16]`, `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`, `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.18]`.

Copyright note: this file paraphrases method and stores page references. It does not reproduce long passages.

## Method Takeaway

Book IV is a major time-lord source. It distributes periods from Fortune and Daimon, breaks periods into years/months/days/hours, and reads transmissions between planets, Ascendant, signs, and Lots. The reusable project rule is not the full computation yet; it is the discipline that timing activates natal promise through a specified anchor and chain of transmission.

## Rule Card IV.1 - Chronocratorship Requires A Starting Anchor

Source: Book IV.1-IV.4, local PDF pp. 71-73.

Use when:

- selecting a time-lord technique;
- deciding whether to start from Fortune, Daimon, Ascendant, or another anchor;
- documenting why a timing method is in scope.

Required chart data:

- selected anchor;
- anchor sign/degree;
- ruler and condition;
- method-specific period table.

Procedure:

1. Name the timing technique.
2. Name the starting anchor before calculating.
3. Record why that anchor is appropriate to the question.
4. Do not mix anchors without explicitly comparing the results.

Guardrail:

Timing begins from an accountable anchor. It is not a free scan of all possible periods.

## Rule Card IV.2 - Fortune And Daimon Separate Material Circumstance From Action

Source: Book IV.4 and IV.7, local PDF pp. 71-75.

Use when:

- timing bodily/material circumstances;
- timing action, intention, reputation, or undertaking;
- comparing Fortune-derived and Daimon-derived chronocratorships.

Required chart data:

- Fortune sign/degree and ruler;
- Daimon sign/degree and ruler;
- period allotment scheme;
- planets configured to the Lots.

Procedure:

1. Use Fortune when the timing question concerns body, livelihood, circumstance, or material fortune.
2. Use Daimon when the timing question concerns action, intent, career movement, or undertaking.
3. Compare rulers and configured planets.
4. Keep the two distributions distinct unless the source method connects them.

Guardrail:

Do not treat Fortune and Daimon as interchangeable timing lots.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/lots-and-fortune.md`
- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* IV.4, IV.7, PDF pp. 71-75]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.16]`.

## Rule Card IV.3 - Transmission Links The Natal Promise To The Period

Source: Book IV.5, IV.13, IV.17-IV.24, local PDF pp. 73, 81, 84-89.

Use when:

- a period is transmitted from one sign/planet to another;
- judging what a time lord activates;
- interpreting a planet-to-planet period.

Required chart data:

- transmitting planet/sign;
- receiving planet/sign;
- natal relationship between them;
- condition of both planets;
- topic houses and Lots involved.

Procedure:

1. Identify transmitter and receiver.
2. Check their natal relationship: co-presence, configuration, aversion, reception, and place.
3. Read the period through the receiving planet's topics and condition.
4. Confirm that the activated topic exists in natal testimony.

Guardrail:

A transmission activates natal structure. It does not create an unsupported topic from scratch.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* IV.5, IV.13, IV.17-IV.24, PDF pp. 73, 81, 84-89]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`.

## Rule Card IV.4 - Period Quality Depends On Place, Sect, Benefics, And Malefics

Source: Book IV.7-IV.17, local PDF pp. 74-84.

Use when:

- judging whether a period is supportive, difficult, mixed, or unstable;
- time lord or receiver is benefic/malefic;
- a planet is in a good/bad place from the anchor.

Required chart data:

- time lord and receiver;
- place from Ascendant and from timing anchor;
- sect;
- benefic/malefic configurations;
- natal condition audits.

Procedure:

1. Audit the time lord natally.
2. Check its place relative to the timing anchor.
3. Add benefic/malefic testimony.
4. Preserve mixed judgment if the same period has support and harm.

Guardrail:

Do not reduce a period to the natural benefic/malefic label alone.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`
- `rules/planet-condition-audit.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* IV.7-IV.17, PDF pp. 74-84]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`.

## Rule Card IV.5 - Annual And Universal Year Frames Are Conditional Timing Layers

Source: Book IV.9-IV.11, local PDF pp. 75-78.

Use when:

- a question is time-bound to a year;
- the method calls for operative year or universal year;
- annual activation needs a Valens cross-check.

Required chart data:

- birth year/date;
- current year/date;
- selected annual method;
- anchor and period table.

Procedure:

1. Determine whether the question requires annual timing.
2. Select one annual frame and document it.
3. Calculate the operative year according to the method.
4. Interpret only after tying the year to natal testimony.

Guardrail:

Status: needs verification. Do not mix annual frames or apply them when the focus is not time-bound.

## Rule Card IV.6 - Place Names Can Be Counted From Timing Anchors

Source: Book IV.12 and IV.15, local PDF pp. 80-82.

Use when:

- interpreting a time-lord distribution from Fortune, Daimon, or another anchor;
- derived places are used in a timing technique;
- III/IX activation appears in timing.

Required chart data:

- timing anchor sign;
- derived place count from that anchor;
- planets and rulers in the derived place.

Procedure:

1. Identify the anchor.
2. Count the derived place from the anchor.
3. Apply the place topic only within the timing method.
4. Compare with the same place counted from the natal Ascendant.

Guardrail:

Derived timing places should not overwrite the natal house framework.

## Rule Card IV.7 - Phases And Transits Modify Period Outcomes

Source: Book IV.14, local PDF p. 82.

Use when:

- a time lord period is active;
- transits or phases repeat natal testimony;
- evaluating why a period becomes more event-like.

Required chart data:

- active time lord;
- natal promise;
- current transit positions;
- phase/visibility data if relevant.

Procedure:

1. Establish the active period first.
2. Check whether transits of relevant planets contact the activated places or rulers.
3. Treat transits as triggers/modifiers of the period.
4. Do not allow transits to replace natal and time-lord evidence.

Guardrail:

Transits are subordinate to activated natal promise in this Valens layer.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* IV.14, PDF p. 82]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`.

## Rule Card IV.8 - Four Lots Can Receive Timing Distributions

Source: Book IV.25, local PDF p. 90.

Use when:

- a Valens distribution explicitly uses multiple Lots;
- a topic requires a Lot beyond Fortune/Daimon;
- timing focus is tied to a specific Lot.

Required chart data:

- the four Lots and their formulas;
- Lot signs/degrees;
- Lot rulers and conditions;
- period scheme.

Procedure:

1. Identify the Lots used by the method.
2. Verify formulas and positions before interpretation.
3. Audit each Lot's ruler and configured witnesses.
4. Keep Lot distributions topic-specific.

Guardrail:

Status: needs verification. Do not expand ordinary readings to four-Lot timing without source-table support.

## Rule Card IV.9 - Subdistributions Need Explicit Period Granularity

Source: Book IV.10-IV.11, IV.26-IV.30, local PDF pp. 76-78, 91-94.

Use when:

- dividing a period into years, months, days, or hours;
- comparing long and short periods;
- building a later timing calculator.

Required chart data:

- base period;
- subdivision table;
- elapsed time;
- receiving planet/sign.

Procedure:

1. Name the base period.
2. Name the subdivision level.
3. Calculate the subdivision without changing the anchor.
4. Interpret the subdivision as a refinement of the same timing chain.

Status: needs verification. Subperiod math belongs in a reference/calculation layer before use in prose.
