## Method Takeaway

Book III is dominated by lifespan, apheta/controller, vital sector, hostile places, critical years, and table-based timing. Most of it is not v1 natal-core material. It should be preserved as a timing/longevity source pool with strict data and ethical constraints.

## Rule Card III.1 - Controller/Apheta Selection Is A Specialist Gate

Source: Book III.1, local PDF pp. 58-59.

Use when:

- researching Valens lifespan methods;
- a later specialist timing module needs apheta/controller selection;
- comparing luminary, Ascendant, and MC roles in ancient longevity doctrine.

Required chart data:

- day/night chart;
- Sun and Moon signs/degrees;
- Ascendant and MC degrees;
- terms/bounds;
- configurations and sect.

Procedure:

1. Evaluate Sun and Moon as possible controlling points.
2. Prefer the luminary that is more appropriately configured, sect-aligned, and operational by the method.
3. If luminaries are unsuitable, inspect Ascendant or MC degree terms.
4. Record controller and houseruler separately.

## Rule Card III.2 - Angular Degrees Can Be Operative Or Inoperative

Source: Book III.2, local PDF p. 59.

Use when:

- a Valens method distinguishes degrees within an angular quadrant;
- exact angular distances are available;
- checking whether a planet near an angle is operative in a lifespan/timing method.

Required chart data:

- exact Ascendant, MC, Descendant, and IC degrees;
- planet degrees;
- quadrant distances.

Procedure:

1. Calculate the degree span from angle to angle in the required direction.
2. Identify the operative portion specified by the method.
3. Mark planets within that operative portion as stronger for that method.
4. Keep this separate from whole-sign angularity unless Phase B integrates it.

## Rule Card III.3 - Vital Sector And Anaeretic Rays Are Lifespan-Specific

Source: Book III.3, local PDF pp. 59-62.

Use when:

- reconstructing Valens length-of-life procedure;
- evaluating destructive rays and hostile terms;
- comparing multiple lifespan methods.

Required chart data:

- apheta/controller;
- houseruler;
- angles and degrees;
- malefic/luminary phase contacts;
- terms and rays.

Procedure:

1. Establish whether the nativity has a controller and houseruler.
2. Determine the vital sector and its degree span.
3. Check anaeretic stars, terms, and rays.
4. Compare the years of apheta and houseruler according to the method.
5. Report uncertainty whenever data or table support is incomplete.

## Rule Card III.4 - Winds, Steps, Motion, And Sect Modify Timing Effectiveness

Source: Book III.4-III.5, local PDF pp. 61-62.

Use when:

- a time-lord or aphetic method checks planetary effectiveness;
- judging whether a planet is rising, setting, retrograde, in proper motion, or sect-aligned.

Required chart data:

- planet motion;
- sect;
- angularity;
- wind/step if method requires;
- relation to apheta or chronocrator.

Procedure:

1. Record the planet's motion and visibility/placement state.
2. Check sect alignment.
3. If the method uses wind/step, record it as a condition modifier.
4. Apply the planet more strongly when rising, angular, proper in motion, and sect-aligned.

## Rule Card III.5 - Hostile Places And Critical Points Are Table-Based Timing Data

Source: Book III.7K-III.11K;8P, local PDF pp. 62-66.

Use when:

- a Valens critical-period method is being reconstructed;
- checking hostile degrees/signs relative to planets, Ascendant, Sun, or Moon;
- comparing critical years across multiple systems.

Required chart data:

- planet and angle degrees;
- term rulers;
- hostile places/tables;
- chronocratorship timing.

Procedure:

1. Identify the natal planet or point whose hostile places are being checked.
2. Determine the opposing/critical degree or term ruler by the table.
3. Check whether the relevant chronocratorship or transit reaches that place.
4. Treat the result as timing testimony only, not natal promise by itself.

## Rule Card III.7 - Fortune Can Enter Length-Of-Life And Critical-Year Methods

Source: Book III.14K;11P-III.15K;12P, local PDF pp. 68-70.

Use when:

- researching Valens lifespan or critical-year methods;
- evaluating whether Fortune's ruler or placement affects duration/timing;
- comparing Fortune-based timing with Ascendant-based timing.

Required chart data:

- Fortune sign and degree;
- Fortune ruler and condition;
- rising times by klima;
- malefic/benefic configurations.

Procedure:

1. Locate Fortune and its ruler.
2. Evaluate whether Fortune is angular, operative, following, or preceding an angle according to the method.
3. Apply star periods/rising times only after table verification.
4. Compare with other lifespan methods rather than using one result alone.

## Rule Card III.8 - Mean Years And Minimum Periods Are Deterministic Timing Tables

Source: Book III.15K;13P, local PDF p. 70.

Use when:

- a timing method explicitly calls for planetary years;
- building a later reference table;
- comparing Valens period values with Brennan or other sources.

Required chart data:

- planet involved;
- selected period type: minimum, mean, or other;
- timing technique using the period.

Procedure:

1. Record the period table and source.
2. Do not mix period types unless the method instructs it.
3. Keep deterministic period tables in reference files, not prose workflow.
