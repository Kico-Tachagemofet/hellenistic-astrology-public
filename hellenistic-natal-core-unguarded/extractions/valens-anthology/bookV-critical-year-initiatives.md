## Method Takeaway

Book V continues timing with crisis-producing places, critical years, operative months/days, lunar inclinations, recast charts, and aphetic transmissions. The most reusable rule is anti-mechanical timing: repeated cycles do not guarantee repeated outcomes unless the active planet, place, and natal promise line up.

## Rule Card V.1 - Crisis-Producing Places Are Timing Triggers, Not Natal Verdicts

Source: Book V.1-V.3, local PDF pp. 95-97.

Use when:

- identifying critical years or crisis-producing signs;
- a timing method flags a dangerous or unstable period;
- checking whether the period has natal support.

Required chart data:

- active year or period;
- crisis-producing place/sign;
- planets and rulers involved;
- natal condition of the activated factors.

Procedure:

1. Identify the critical sign/place by the chosen method.
2. Check whether natal malefic/benefic testimony supports the forecast.
3. Treat the critical marker as a timing trigger.
4. Report uncertainty when the natal promise is weak or contradictory.

## Rule Card V.2 - Recasting A Horoscope Is A Separate Timing Layer

Source: Book V.4K;3P-V.5K, local PDF p. 97.

Use when:

- Valens calls for a recast chart for propitious/impropitious times;
- evaluating initiatives or temporal changes;
- comparing natal and recast testimony.

Required chart data:

- natal chart;
- recast date/time;
- active period;
- relevant anchor.

Procedure:

1. Build or import the recast chart only after selecting the timing question.
2. Compare recast placements to natal anchors and active period rulers.
3. Use the recast chart as temporal testimony, not as a new natal chart.

## Rule Card V.3 - Operative Month And Day Require Explicit Formula Paths

Source: Book V.6K;4P-V.8K;5P, local PDF pp. 97-102.

Use when:

- timing a month or day inside an active year;
- a method requires current Sun, natal Moon, Ascendant, or lunar inclination;
- distinguishing auspicious and inauspicious days.

Required chart data:

- current date;
- current Sun and Moon;
- natal Moon and Ascendant;
- selected monthly/day formula;
- table values if used.

Procedure:

1. Identify whether the method is monthly or daily.
2. Record the formula path.
3. Calculate the operative sign/planet.
4. Interpret only after checking natal and annual activation.

## Rule Card V.4 - Twelve-Year Repetition Is Not Mechanical

Source: Book V.9K;6P, local PDF pp. 102-105.

Use when:

- a timing cycle repeats by sign interval;
- comparing similar periods in a chart;
- preventing simplistic recurrence claims.

Required chart data:

- current period and previous matching interval;
- active planets/signs;
- natal configurations;
- benefic/malefic condition and transits.

Procedure:

1. Identify the repeated interval.
2. Compare active planets and places in both periods.
3. Check whether benefic/malefic testimony changed.
4. Explain difference through the full timing chain rather than assuming recurrence.

Guardrail:

Do not say "the same thing happens every twelve years" unless the operative chain is materially the same.

## Rule Card V.5 - Aphetic Points Must Match The Question And Sect

Source: Book V.11K;7P, local PDF pp. 108-109.

Use when:

- selecting a starting point for transmission;
- comparing Sun, Moon, Ascendant, Fortune, or other aphetic anchors;
- interpreting a period with multiple possible starting points.

Required chart data:

- chart sect;
- candidate aphetic points;
- condition and place of candidates;
- timing method.

Procedure:

1. List eligible aphetic points.
2. Apply the source's preference rules, including sect where stated.
3. Choose the anchor that fits the method and question.
4. Record rejected anchors if they were plausible.

Guardrail:

Anchor selection is part of the evidence chain and must be visible.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* V.11K;7P, PDF pp. 108-109]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.18]`.

## Rule Card V.6 - Example Nativities Are Validation, Not General Rules

Source: Book V.10K and V.12K;8P, local PDF pp. 106-112.

Use when:

- checking whether an extracted timing method is applied consistently;
- comparing Valens' examples to rule cards;
- looking for edge cases.

Procedure:

1. Treat example nativities as demonstrations of the method.
2. Extract only the procedural lesson.
3. Do not generalize an example's outcome without matching chart structure.

Guardrail:

Example outcomes are not portable delineation snippets.
