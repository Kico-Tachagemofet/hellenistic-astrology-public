Range: local PDF pp. 113-123.

Brennan cross-ref: `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.7]`, `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`.

## Method Takeaway

Book VI refines timing through degree intervals, chronocrator contacts, transits, planet-quality analogies, and subdistributions. It reinforces the project rule that transits become vigorous when tied to active time-lord rulers and activated places.

## Rule Card VI.1 - Degree-Interval Contacts Refine Chronocratorship

Source: Book VI.1K;2P, local PDF pp. 114-116.

Use when:

- a period is already active;
- the source method requires exact degree intervals;
- timing needs more precision than sign-level distribution.

Required chart data:

- active chronocrator;
- exact degrees of relevant planets/points;
- interval calculation method;
- natal configurations.

Procedure:

1. Establish the active chronocrator.
2. Calculate degree intervals between active and natal factors.
3. Check contacts to activated places or rulers.
4. Use degree intervals as refinement, not as the first timing layer.

## Rule Card VI.2 - Planetary Quality Analogies Explain Weight, Not Outcome

Source: Book VI.2K;3P-VI.3K;4P, local PDF pp. 116-117.

Use when:

- explaining why malefic or benefic testimony may dominate a mixture;
- comparing intrinsic planetary nature with contextual condition;
- translating severe source language into audit logic.

Required chart data:

- planets involved;
- condition, sect, and place;
- configurations and timing activation.

Procedure:

1. Record the planet's natural benefic/malefic quality.
2. Check whether condition amplifies, mitigates, or redirects it.
3. Treat stronger malefic activity as a weighting factor, not automatic disaster.
4. Report mixed states when benefic and malefic testimony both operate.

Guardrail:

Natural malefic/benefic status is not enough. Context decides manifestation.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/planet-condition-audit.md`
- `rules/delineation-output-rules.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* VI.2K;3P-VI.3K;4P, PDF pp. 116-117]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.7]`.

## Rule Card VI.3 - Transits Are Vigorous When Time-Lord Masters Are Involved

Source: Book VI.4K;5P, local PDF p. 117.

Use when:

- evaluating a transit during an active period;
- the transiting planet rules or masters the chronocratorship;
- a transit contacts an activated place, ruler, or Lot.

Required chart data:

- active chronocrator/time lord;
- transiting planet positions;
- activated natal places;
- natal condition of transiting/time-lord planets.

Procedure:

1. Identify the active time-lord master.
2. Check whether that planet transits activated places or aspects their rulers.
3. Treat these transits as more operational than unrelated transits.
4. Interpret through natal promise and period context.

Guardrail:

Do not read every transit equally. Valens privileges transits tied to the active timing chain.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* VI.4K;5P, PDF p. 117]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`.

## Rule Card VI.4 - Long Cycles Can Be Subdistributed To Planets

Source: Book VI.5K;6P-VI.7, local PDF pp. 117-120.

Use when:

- dividing a major period into subperiods;
- identifying monthly/daily rulers inside a larger distribution;
- building a later timing calculator.

Required chart data:

- base distribution cycle;
- planet period table;
- elapsed years/months/days;
- order of planetary transmission.

Procedure:

1. Calculate the major distribution.
2. Select the subdistribution level.
3. Assign subperiods to planets in the source order.
4. Interpret the subperiod planet through natal condition and active period context.

## Rule Card VI.5 - Full-Moon And Horoscopic Gnomon Methods Are Specialist Data Procedures

Source: Book VI.8-VI.9, local PDF pp. 120-123.

Use when:

- reconstructing Valens length-of-life or conception calculations;
- a later specialist module needs horoscopic gnomon logic;
- comparing Book VI with Book I and Book III prenatal methods.

Required chart data:

- full-moon or lunation data;
- Ascendant degree;
- Moon degree and motion;
- table values.

Procedure:

1. Identify the specific gnomon method.
2. Record input values and source table.
3. Keep the derived result separate from natal chart data.
4. Cross-check against other Valens methods before treating it as usable.
