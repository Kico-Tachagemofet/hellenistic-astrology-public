# Valens Extraction - Book II Places, Lots, And Family Topics

Source: Vettius Valens, `Anthologies`, Book II, Mark Riley translation.

Range: local PDF pp. 25-57.

Brennan cross-ref: `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.8]`, `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.10]`, `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.13]`, `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.16]`.

Copyright note: this file paraphrases method and stores page references. It does not reproduce long passages.

## Method Takeaway

Book II is a major bridge between house/place doctrine, Lot doctrine, configurations, status topics, family topics, marriage/children, travel, illness, and death. For this project it is the strongest Valens witness for treating Fortune as a derived Ascendant and for handling the named places as structured topic fields rather than isolated placements.

## Rule Card II.1 - Triplicity/Triangle Rulers Are Sect-Dependent

Source: Book II.1-II.2, local PDF pp. 25-26.

Use when:

- evaluating triplicity rulers;
- comparing elemental triangles by day/night sect;
- building a later source cross-check against dignity tables.

Required chart data:

- sect;
- sign/elemental triangle;
- planet occupying or ruling the relevant sign.

Procedure:

1. Identify the triangle/triplicity of the sign.
2. Select rulers according to the chart's sect and Valens' order.
3. Treat the selected ruler as a support layer for condition or timing.
4. Cross-check with the project's canonical dignity table before any rule update.

## Rule Card II.2 - Fortune Can Function As A Derived Ascendant

Source: Book II.3 and II.17K;18P, local PDF pp. 26 and 34.

Use when:

- judging bodily, material, circumstantial, livelihood, or fortune topics;
- comparing the natal Ascendant with Fortune;
- preparing timing from Fortune.

Required chart data:

- Fortune sign and degree;
- Fortune ruler and condition;
- planets co-present with or configured to Fortune;
- places counted from Fortune.

Procedure:

1. Calculate or import Fortune.
2. Treat Fortune's sign as a derived Ascendant when the technique calls for it.
3. Judge planets and places relative to Fortune as a secondary house system.
4. Compare Fortune-derived testimony with Ascendant-derived testimony.
5. If they disagree, preserve the tension instead of averaging.

Guardrail:

Fortune is not a planet. Its place, ruler, witnesses, and derived houses carry the judgment.

## Rule Card II.3 - Ascendant Ruler, Fortune Ruler, And Lot Holder Must Be Compared

Source: Book II.3-II.4, local PDF pp. 26-27.

Use when:

- both Ascendant and Fortune are relevant;
- a planet rules the Ascendant, Fortune, or another major Lot;
- determining which planet carries the topic's operational burden.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant sign and ruler;
- Fortune sign and ruler;
- planet holding Ascendant or Lot by rulership/co-presence/configuration;
- condition audits for the candidate planets.

Procedure:

1. List the Ascendant ruler and Fortune ruler separately.
2. Audit each planet once, then reuse the audit for each role.
3. Record whether one planet carries multiple roles.
4. Do not count the same planet's repeated role as independent evidence unless a distinct configured witness or derived-place testimony is added.

Guardrail:

This supports the current same-planet multi-role discipline.

## Rule Card II.4 - Named Places Are Topic Fields With Quality

Source: Book II.5-II.15, local PDF pp. 27-30.

Use when:

- assigning topics to the twelve places;
- judging whether a planet in a place can act effectively;
- explaining good/bad daimon, good/bad fortune, God/Goddess, and angular places.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant sign;
- whole-sign place;
- planets in the place;
- place ruler and configured benefics/malefics.

Procedure:

1. Identify the whole-sign place from the Ascendant.
2. Record the place name and baseline quality.
3. Judge planets in the place by condition, not by place name alone.
4. Combine the place's topic with ruler-chain and configuration testimony.

Guardrail:

Place names are not self-sufficient outcomes. They define topical fields and operating conditions.

## Rule Card II.5 - IX And III Carry God/Goddess And Divinatory Testimony

Source: Book II.7K;8P and II.13K;14P, local PDF pp. 28-29.

Use when:

- reading religious, temple, prophetic, dream, divinatory, foreign, or sacred-office themes;
- building v2 spiritual/divinatory focus;
- evaluating III/IX activation in timing.

Required chart data:

- planets in III/IX;
- rulers of III/IX;
- configurations to Ascendant, Fortune, Daimon, luminaries, or Mercury;
- sect and condition of involved planets.

Procedure:

1. Treat IX as the place of God and III as the place of Goddess/Moon.
2. Link religious/divinatory topics only when supported by planets, rulers, Lots, or timing.
3. Check Mercury, luminaries, and relevant Lot testimony for reinforcement.
4. Report whether the testimony is vocational, devotional, intellectual, travel/foreign, or timing-specific.

Guardrail:

Do not turn any III/IX placement into a spirituality claim without a full evidence chain.

## Rule Card II.6 - Configurations Modify Prosperity And Poverty Claims

Source: Book II.16K;17P, local PDF pp. 30-34.

Use when:

- judging prosperity, poverty, support, or reversal;
- a planet or Lot is configured by benefics/malefics;
- interpreting trine, sextile, and opposition testimony.

Required chart data:

- sign configuration;
- degree closeness if available;
- benefic/malefic condition;
- relevant Lot or house topic.

Procedure:

1. Identify the configuration by sign.
2. Check whether benefics or malefics are involved and how they are conditioned.
3. Connect the configuration to a relevant topic anchor, such as Fortune, Ascendant, or a house ruler.
4. Preserve mixed outcomes when a configuration gives both support and instability.

Guardrail:

Do not treat configuration type alone as sufficient. Planet condition and topic anchor are required.

- `rules/delineation-output-rules.md`

Provenance: `[Valens *Anth.* II.16K;17P, PDF pp. 30-34]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.9]`.

## Rule Card II.7 - Daimon/Spirit And Accomplishment Are Actional Timing Anchors

Source: Book II.19K;20P and II.20K;21P, local PDF p. 35.

Use when:

- separating material fortune from action/undertaking;
- judging outcomes of actions;
- deriving the 11th place from Fortune or Daimon.

Required chart data:

- Fortune and Daimon signs/degrees;
- rulers of Fortune and Daimon;
- derived 11th from Fortune/Daimon if the method calls for it;
- configured planets.

Procedure:

1. Read Fortune as bodily/material circumstance.
2. Read Daimon as actional/intentional undertaking.
3. Examine derived places, especially accomplishment-style places, when the technique calls for it.
4. Compare the two Lots before producing a final judgment.

Guardrail:

Do not blur Fortune and Daimon into one generic "luck" point.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

## Rule Card II.8 - Topic Lots Require Formula, Topic Need, And Ruler Audit

Source: Book II.23K;24P-II.25K;26P and II.29K;30P, local PDF pp. 39-41.

Use when:

- a topic-specific Lot is explicitly relevant;
- the formula is known and source-cited;
- ordinary house/ruler evidence needs a focused cross-check.

Required chart data:

- Lot formula;
- sect if formula is sect-dependent;
- Lot sign/degree;
- Lot ruler and condition;
- configured planets.

Procedure:

1. Record the Lot name and formula.
2. Calculate the Lot.
3. Audit the Lot's place, ruler, co-presence, and configurations.
4. Keep topic Lots below Ascendant, topic house, Fortune/Daimon, and time-lord evidence unless repeated testimony elevates them.

Guardrail:

Status: needs verification for exact formulas before automation. Do not clutter ordinary readings with many topic Lots.

## Rule Card II.9 - Family Topics Need Separate Anchors

Source: Book II.30K;31P-II.40, local PDF pp. 43-54.

Use when:

- reading parents, marriage, children, siblings, or family separation;
- determining whether a family topic has enough testimony.

Required chart data:

- relevant place and ruler;
- luminary testimony where applicable;
- topic Lot if formula/source is available;
- benefic/malefic configurations;
- sect and planet condition.

Procedure:

1. Identify the family topic's primary house/place.
2. Add luminaries or natural significators only when the source calls for them.
3. Use topic Lots only with formula and source.
4. Check benefic/malefic support or harm.
5. Keep family topics separated rather than blending all family testimony into one claim.

Guardrail:

Do not infer family outcomes from a single placement. This material is topic-specific and often severe.

- `rules/significator-hierarchy.md`

## Rule Card II.10 - Illness, Injury, Violent Death, And Historical Social Categories Require Ethical Containment

Source: Book II.34K;35P, II.36K;37P, II.41, local PDF pp. 46-57.

Use when:

- cataloguing source material;
- a later specialist module explicitly asks for medical, death, or historical social-status doctrine;
- building ethical boundaries for client-facing output.

Required chart data:

- exact topic request;
- relevant place/ruler/Lot;
- malefic/benefic configurations;
- timing activation if the claim is event-like.

Procedure:

1. Preserve the source as historical doctrine.
2. Mark it as specialist and ethically sensitive.
3. Do not include it in ordinary natal synthesis.
4. If used in research, keep claims conditional and evidence-chain based.

Guardrail:

Do not operationalize violent-death, bodily anomaly, enslavement/free-status, or deterministic illness claims in v1 client-facing readings.
