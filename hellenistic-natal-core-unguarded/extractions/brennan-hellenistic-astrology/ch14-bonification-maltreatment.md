# Brennan Extraction — Chapter 14 Bonification And Maltreatment

Source: Chris Brennan, `Hellenistic Astrology: The Study of Fate and Fortune`, Chapter 14.

Scope:

- conditions by which benefics improve a planet/topic;
- conditions by which malefics harm a planet/topic;
- overcoming, opposition/trine, counteraction, enclosure, adherence, striking with a ray, engagement;
- reception as mitigation.

Copyright note: this file paraphrases method and stores page references. It does not reproduce long passages.

## Chapter Range

- Printed pp. 461-493.
- Approx. PDF pp. 488-520.

## Method Takeaway

A planet is not judged only by sign and house. The quality of its output is strongly modified by which planets act upon it and whether those acting planets are benefics or malefics, of sect or contrary to sect, and received or unsupported.

For the future skill, this chapter feeds the external testimony layer of the condition audit.

## Rule Card 14.1 — Bonification And Maltreatment As Testimony Layer

Source: Ch. 14, printed pp. 461-466; PDF pp. 488-493.

Use when:

- a significator has already been identified;
- evaluating whether that significator can produce its topic constructively;
- distinguishing capacity from harm/support.

Required chart data:

- relevant benefic/malefic positions;
- aspects/configurations to the significator;
- sect status of benefics/malefics;
- dignity/condition of the benefic or malefic providing testimony.

Procedure:

1. Identify the target significator.
2. Identify benefic testimony to it.
3. Identify malefic testimony to it.
4. Judge testimony strength by configuration type, sect, degree proximity, and reception.
5. Add testimony to the planet's net condition rather than replacing the whole interpretation.

Output categories:

- bonified;
- maltreated;
- mixed testimony;
- protected from harm;
- harmed with mitigation;
- unsupported/isolated.

## Rule Card 14.2 — Overcoming / Superior Testimony

Source: Ch. 14, overcoming section, printed pp. 466-470; PDF pp. 493-497.

Use when:

- one planet is in a superior position relative to another by sign-based configuration;
- deciding which planet acts more forcefully on the other.

Procedure:

1. Identify sign-based configuration.
2. Determine whether one planet overcomes the other from the superior position.
3. If a malefic overcomes the significator, mark strong pressure or maltreatment.
4. If a benefic overcomes the significator, mark strong support or bonification.
5. Modify by sect and reception.

Guardrail:

Do not treat all aspects as symmetrical. Direction and superiority matter.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/aspect-reception-rules.md`

## Rule Card 14.3 — Opposition, Trine, And Testimony Quality

Source: Ch. 14, opposition/trine section, printed pp. 471-472; PDF pp. 498-499.

Use when:

- benefic/malefic testimony occurs by opposition or trine;
- determining whether the configuration is supportive, hostile, or mixed.

Procedure:

1. Identify configuration type.
2. Read malefic opposition as potentially damaging or obstructive.
3. Read benefic trine as potentially supportive or protective.
4. Still audit the acting planet's sect and condition.

Destination rule file:

- `rules/aspect-reception-rules.md`

## Rule Card 14.4 — Counteraction

Source: Ch. 14, counteraction section, printed pp. 472-474; PDF pp. 499-501.

Use when:

- a planet is ruled by a poorly placed malefic;
- the domicile lord of the significator's sign becomes an indirect source of harm.

Procedure:

1. Identify the target planet's domicile lord.
2. Audit that lord.
3. If the lord is a badly situated malefic, mark counteraction or indirect maltreatment.
4. Check for reception/mitigation before final judgment.

## Rule Card 14.5 — Enclosure

Source: Ch. 14, enclosure section, printed pp. 474-477; PDF pp. 501-504.

Use when:

- a planet is bounded or contained by benefics or malefics by degree or sign logic depending on source standard;
- evaluating containment, protection, pressure, or trapping.

Procedure:

1. Identify whether planets on either side of the target create an enclosure.
2. If enclosed by malefics, mark strong containment or maltreatment.
3. If enclosed by benefics, mark protection or support.
4. If mixed, explain the mixed containment rather than forcing one outcome.
5. Note uncertainty if exact degree standards are unclear.

## Rule Card 14.6 — Adherence, Striking With A Ray, Engagement

Source: Ch. 14, printed pp. 477-490; PDF pp. 504-517.

Use when:

- a planet is closely applying to or being acted upon by another planet;
- event-like judgment depends on whether testimony is forming, exact, or separating.

Procedure:

1. Identify whether the target planet is applying to a benefic or malefic.
2. Identify whether a benefic/malefic is acting by ray/aspect onto the target.
3. Distinguish close degree-based testimony from looser sign-based testimony.
4. Treat applying relationships as more active for unfolding events.
5. Weigh malefic close application as pressure unless reception or other mitigation exists.
6. Weigh benefic close application as support unless the benefic is weak or contrary to the topic.

Guardrail:

Do not over-specify the exact ancient technical term if source ambiguity remains. For the skill, the operational question is: who is closely acting on whom, and is the acting planet benefic/malefic, strong/weak, received/unreceived?

Destination rule file:

- `rules/aspect-reception-rules.md`

## Rule Card 14.7 — Reception As Mitigation Or Amplification

Source: Ch. 14, reception section, printed pp. 491-493; PDF pp. 518-520.

Use when:

- a planet is in another planet's domicile, exaltation, or other dignity and configured with that ruler;
- deciding whether harmful testimony is softened or helpful testimony is strengthened.

Procedure:

1. Identify whether the acting/receiving planets have a dignity relationship.
2. Check whether the host planet receives the guest by sign-based rulership or other dignity.
3. If a malefic harms a planet but receives it, reduce severity or explain structured difficulty.
4. If a benefic helps a planet and receives it, strengthen the support.
5. If no reception, leave the testimony rawer and less integrated.
