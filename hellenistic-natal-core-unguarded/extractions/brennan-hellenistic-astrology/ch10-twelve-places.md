## Method Takeaway

The Hellenistic house layer should be modeled as `places` first, with whole sign houses as the main topic skeleton.

Quadrant angles and the degree of the Midheaven remain important, but they should not replace the whole sign topic map in the first pass. They act as strength, visibility, angularity, and public-action witnesses.

## Rule Card 10.1 — Whole Sign Places As Topic Skeleton

Source: Ch. 10, whole sign house system section, printed pp. 319-323; PDF pp. 346-350.

Use when:

- constructing the natal data contract;
- assigning planets to topical houses;
- finding house rulers and ruler chains.

Required chart data:

- Ascendant sign and degree;
- all planet signs and degrees;
- whole sign place number for each planet;
- quadrant MC degree as a separate field if available.

Procedure:

1. Use the rising sign as the entire first place.
2. Count the rest of the signs in zodiacal order to establish the twelve places.
3. Assign planets to places by sign, not by quadrant cusp.
4. Use this map for topics, house rulers, and derived houses.
5. Record MC degree separately as an angular/public action witness.

Guardrail:

Do not silently switch to quadrant houses for topic delineation. If a source or user chart uses another house system, label the assumption and convert topic logic carefully.

## Rule Card 10.2 — Angular Triads And Manifestation Strength

Source: Ch. 10, angles/pivots and angular triads, printed pp. 325-332; PDF pp. 352-359.

Use when:

- judging how directly a planet manifests;
- distinguishing topic from strength;
- explaining angular/succedent/cadent placement.

Procedure:

1. Identify the four pivots/angles: Ascendant, Midheaven, Descendant, IC.
2. Group places around angular triads: angular, succedent, declining/cadent.
3. Use angularity as visibility and manifestation strength, not as the topic itself.
4. Treat planets in angular places as more prominent and capable of acting visibly.
5. Treat succedent places as sustaining, following, accumulating, or supporting.
6. Treat cadent/declining places as less direct, more hidden, preparatory, declining, or displaced.

Guardrail:

A cadent planet is not automatically meaningless. It may still matter strongly if it rules the Ascendant/topic, is a time lord, has dignity, or receives strong testimony.

## Rule Card 10.3 — Good/Bad And Advantageous/Disadvantageous Places

Source: Ch. 10, good/bad and busy/advantageous places, printed pp. 332-336; PDF pp. 359-363.

Use when:

- evaluating whether a place is conducive to the native;
- adding environmental quality to planets or rulers placed there.

Procedure:

1. Mark places that are configured to the Ascendant as more directly supportive or visible to the native.
2. Mark aversion to the Ascendant as a major reason certain places are more difficult.
3. Distinguish difficult place quality from planet condition and topic result.
4. Always check mitigation before strong negative claims.

Working principle:

```text
A place can be difficult because it does not directly witness the Ascendant or because it belongs to a declining/dissipating triad.
But a planet there can still become important through rulership, dignity, timing, or testimony.
```

## Rule Card 10.4 — Planetary Joys As Conceptual Architecture

Source: Ch. 10, joys section, printed pp. 336-340; PDF pp. 363-367.

Use when:

- explaining why certain houses have their themes;
- adding symbolic nuance to a house/planet placement;
- building occult or spiritual extension later.

Procedure:

1. Record planetary joys as a conceptual layer, not the first judgment layer.
2. Use joys to explain resonance between planet and place.
3. Do not override ruler-chain and condition audit with joys alone.

Working joy list:

- Mercury joys in the 1st.
- Moon joys in the 3rd.
- Venus joys in the 5th.
- Mars joys in the 6th.
- Sun joys in the 9th.
- Jupiter joys in the 11th.
- Saturn joys in the 12th.

## Rule Card 10.5 — House Significations Need Source-Layer Caution

Source: Ch. 10, significations and survey sections, printed pp. 340-363; PDF pp. 367-390.

Use when:

- assigning topic meanings to houses;
- preventing modern meanings from contaminating the Hellenistic layer.

Procedure:

1. Use Hellenistic significations as the first-pass topic list.
2. Mark later/modern meanings separately if added.
3. Do not import modern house meanings automatically, especially where the tradition differs.
4. Keep sex, psychology, and modern career meanings source-labeled when they differ from Hellenistic place meanings.
5. Use derived houses only after the primary topic map is clear.

Guardrail:

House meanings are not all from one symbolic rationale. Some arise from angularity, aversion, planetary joys, derived houses, or older topic lists. Keep the source layer visible.

## Rule Card 10.6 — Planets In Places Require Role And Condition

Source: Ch. 10, interpreting planets in places, printed pp. 362-363; PDF pp. 389-390.

Use when:

- reading a planet located in a house;
- preventing cookbook placement readings.

Procedure:

1. Identify the place topic.
2. Identify the planet's natural significations.
3. Identify whether the planet also rules relevant houses.
4. Audit planet condition.
5. Check aspects, reception, and time activation.
6. Interpret the planet's presence as a local actor in the place, not as the whole topic judgment.

Guardrail:

A planet in a place is not the same as the ruler of that place. Keep tenant logic and ruler logic separate.
