# Dorotheus Extraction — Book I Births And General Delineation

Source: Dorotheus of Sidon, *Carmen Astrologicum: The 'Umar al-Tabari Translation*, trans. Benjamin Dykes, 2017, Book I, printed pp. 61-117.

Transmission note: source is Dorotheus through 'Umar al-Tabari, translated by Dykes. Every rule below is a paraphrase, not a quotation.

Brennan cross-ref: Ch.7 planets, Ch.8 signs/dignities, Ch.10 places, Ch.13 rulers, Ch.16 Lots, Ch.17 timing by later activation.

## Method Takeaway

Book I is not just a list of natal outcomes. Its reusable method is stacked testimony:

```text
topic anchor -> triplicity lords / ruler -> place quality -> visibility/motion -> benefic/malefic testimony -> Lot cross-check
```

For the skill, Book I is valuable because it confirms that a topic should not be read from a single placement. Dorotheus repeatedly cross-checks luminaries, triplicity rulers, topical Lots, rulers of Lots, and good/bad places before judgment.

## Rule Card I.1 — Foundational Data Before Judgment

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.1.1-14 and I.2.1-2 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 61-64]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.8]`

Use when:

- preparing any Dorothean-style natal extraction;
- deciding whether a planet has sign-level authority or weakness.

Required data:

- planet signs/degrees;
- domicile rulers;
- exaltation/fall degrees;
- triplicity rulers by sect;
- sign category if relevant;
- whether exact degrees are available.

Procedure:

1. Record the seven planets by sign and degree before topic reading.
2. Record sign lords and triplicity lords.
3. Record domicile and exaltation/fall where exact dignity claims matter.
4. Treat these as inputs to later condition audit, not as standalone delineations.

Guardrail:

Do not begin with a topic prediction before the sign-ruler and dignity frame has been established.

## Rule Card I.2 — Place Preferability As Accidental Power

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.5.1-5 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 66-67]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.10]`

Use when:

- auditing whether a planet has the ability to act in a topic;
- ranking testimony by angularity and place quality.

Required data:

- whole sign place from Ascendant;
- angular/succeedent/cadent status;
- whether the place is counted among approved or difficult places.

Procedure:

1. Identify whether the planet or Lot ruler is in a stake, follows a stake, or falls away.
2. Treat approved/good places as increasing operative capacity.
3. Treat worst places as weakening or obscuring testimony unless strong mitigation appears.
4. Use place quality as a strength modifier, not as the whole interpretation.

Guardrail:

Do not let a planet's natural benefic/malefic label override place quality and condition.

## Rule Card I.3 — Planet Condition Combines Dignity, Sect, Visibility, Motion, And Place

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.6.1-7 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 67-68]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.7]`

Use when:

- auditing any planet that carries topic, Lot, or timing responsibility.

Required data:

- sect;
- dignity;
- place quality;
- relation to Sun/under-rays state;
- retrograde/direct motion;
- benefic/malefic identity.

Procedure:

1. First check whether the planet is in its own house, exaltation, or triplicity.
2. Check whether it is in a strong or weak place.
3. Check whether it is hidden under the Sun's rays, emerging, eastern/western, or retrograde.
4. Judge malefics by sect context: Saturn is harsher by night, Mars harsher by day.
5. Preserve mixed testimony when a planet has dignity but poor visibility or strong place but poor sect condition.

Guardrail:

No single label is sufficient. Dorotheus's condition logic is cumulative.

Destination:

- `rules/planet-condition-audit.md`

## Rule Card I.4 — Upbringing / Life Support Uses Ascendant Triplicity Lords

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.3.1-8, I.4.1-11, I.7.1-26 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 64-70]` `[new to Dorotheus]`

Use when:

- reading early-life support, survival, or foundation topics in a source-critical way;
- checking whether the Ascendant's condition is supported or damaged.

Required data:

- Ascendant sign and triplicity rulers by sect;
- condition of first, second, and third triplicity lords of the Ascendant;
- benefic/malefic testimony to Ascendant and Moon;
- Lot of Fortune and its ruler if Ascendant testimony is heavily damaged.

Procedure:

1. Start with the Ascendant and its triplicity lords.
2. If at least one triplicity lord is strong, in its own share, or in a strong place, treat that as life-supporting testimony.
3. If malefics afflict the Ascendant or luminaries, do not conclude from that alone; check whether Ascendant triplicity lords or Fortune repair the testimony.
4. Use Lot of Fortune as fallback when the Ascendant frame is too damaged to carry the judgment cleanly.
5. Record whether testimony is supportive, mixed, or damaged.

Guardrail:

This material is historically framed around infant survival and upbringing. Do not turn it into a modern fatalistic prediction.

Destination:

- `rules/significator-hierarchy.md`
- `rules/planet-condition-audit.md`
- `rules/lots-and-fortune.md`

## Rule Card I.5 — Fortune And Religion As Secondary Upbringing Cross-Checks

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.9.1-2 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed p. 72]` `[new to Dorotheus]` `[needs verification]`

Use when:

- Dorotheus explicitly calls for a fallback from Ascendant testimony to Lots.

Required data:

- Lot of Fortune;
- "Lot of Religion" formula and position, if verified;
- Moon's relation to either Lot;
- Lot rulers.

Procedure:

1. Use Fortune as the stable fallback because its formula is already supported elsewhere.
2. Treat the Lot of Religion as a source note until formula and scope are verified.
3. Check Moon contact with the relevant Lot only as supporting testimony.

Guardrail:

Do not import a "Lot of Religion" rule into the skill until the formula and transmission status are checked.

Destination:

## Rule Card I.6 — Way Of Life Requires Topic Anchor Plus Condition

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.12.1-11 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 77-78]` `[new to Dorotheus]`

- converting broad "condition of life" material into a non-cookbook method.

Required data:

- Ascendant;
- luminaries;
- Moon phase/light;
- tenth from Moon where relevant;
- topic-relevant rulers and witnesses.

Procedure:

1. Do not read way of life from a single planet.
2. Build a topic stack from Ascendant/luminary condition, supporting places, and rulers.
3. Treat increasing/decreasing lunar light and northern/southern condition as condition modifiers.
4. Preserve the frame as "general condition" unless a specific topic is requested.

Guardrail:

Avoid converting historically specific social outcomes into modern character claims.

## Rule Card I.7 — Parents: Father And Mother Use Separate Significator Stacks

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.13.1-27 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 78-83]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.13]`

Use when:

- reading parents as a natal topic.

Required data:

- Sun and its triplicity lords for father;
- Moon and its triplicity lords for mother;
- sect;
- place quality of luminaries and triplicity lords;
- benefic/malefic testimony.

Procedure:

1. Read father from Sun plus the Sun's triplicity lords.
2. Read mother from Moon plus the Moon's triplicity lords.
3. In day/night contexts, check whether the sect light or contrary light is more relevant.
4. Judge parent condition from the luminary and its rulers together.
5. Use malefic testimony as pressure only after checking whether the parent significator has support.

Guardrail:

Do not use one luminary alone to describe an entire parent topic.

Destination:

- `rules/significator-hierarchy.md`

## Rule Card I.8 — Parent Lots Supplement Luminaries

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.14.1-6 and I.15.1 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 83-84]` `[new to Dorotheus]`

Use when:

- a parents reading needs a Lot cross-check;
- the source provides calculated Lot positions.

Required data:

- Lot of fathers;
- Lot of mother;
- Lot rulers;
- planets configured to each Lot;
- luminary parent stack from Rule I.7.

Procedure:

1. Calculate or receive the parent Lot with source/formula recorded.
2. Identify sign, place, and ruler of the Lot.
3. Audit Lot ruler and witnesses.
4. Compare Lot testimony to Sun/Moon testimony.
5. Treat agreement as reinforcement and disagreement as mixed testimony, not automatic contradiction.

Guardrail:

Do not introduce parent Lots if formulas or birth time are uncertain.

Destination:

- `rules/lots-and-fortune.md`
- `rules/significator-hierarchy.md`

## Rule Card I.9 — Parental Separation / Fourth Stake As Household Anchor

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.16.1-21 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 84-86]` `[new to Dorotheus]`

Use when:

- checking parental cohesion, separation, or household condition in historical natal topics.

Required data:

- Sun/Moon condition;
- parent Lots;
- fourth stake and its ruler;
- benefic/malefic testimony to the fourth.

Procedure:

1. Treat the fourth stake as the household/family-ground anchor.
2. Compare luminary condition with the fourth stake and parent Lots.
3. Treat malefics to luminaries, Lots, or fourth stake as separation/damage testimony.
4. Use benefics and strong rulers as mitigation.

Guardrail:

This is sensitive family material. Use only when the user asks for parent/family topics and keep confidence explicit.

## Rule Card I.10 — Inheritance Uses Parent Stack Plus Asset Witnesses

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.18.1-13 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 88-89]` `[new to Dorotheus]`

Use when:

- reading inheritance or assets from parents.

Required data:

- parent significator stack;
- second place and its ruler;
- Lot of fathers/mother where relevant;
- Jupiter/Saturn and benefic/malefic witnesses;
- topic house from the relevant parent.

Procedure:

1. Start with the relevant parent significator.
2. Check asset indicators and second-place logic.
3. Check whether the Lot ruler is in a good or bad place.
4. Look for benefic testimony supporting transmission of assets.
5. Preserve uncertainty when parent condition and asset condition disagree.

Guardrail:

Do not make concrete financial predictions without timing activation and repeated testimony.

- reading sibling topics.

Required data:

- third-place topic frame;
- Moon and Mercury condition;
- Lot of siblings and its ruler;
- benefic/malefic testimony to Lot and ruler.

Procedure:

1. Use the ordinary sibling house/topic first.
2. Cross-check Moon/Mercury and water-sign indicators when the source calls for them.
3. Calculate/audit the Lot of siblings if formula is available.
4. Read sibling affection from relation between Lot ruler and Lot witnesses.

Do not use sibling-counting formulas as a default skill behavior.

## Rule Card I.12 — Good Fortune And Assets Require Multiple Anchors

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.24.1-8, I.27.1-8, I.28.1-36 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 98-112]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.16]`

Use when:

- reading wealth, status, or broad good fortune.

Required data:

- sect light and its triplicity lords;
- Lot of Fortune and ruler;
- second place and ruler;
- angle/succeedent lords;
- benefic/malefic testimony;
- visibility and place quality.

Procedure:

1. Begin with the sect light and its triplicity lords for broad life fortune.
2. Audit Lot of Fortune, its ruler, co-present planets, and configured planets.
3. Audit second place and second ruler for assets.
4. Check whether benefics support the Lot/second/rulers or malefics dominate them.
5. Report mixed outcomes when one anchor is strong and another damaged.

Guardrail:

Do not collapse Fortune, second-house assets, and status into one undifferentiated "money" claim.

Destination:

- `rules/lots-and-fortune.md`
- `rules/significator-hierarchy.md`
- `rules/house-topic-framework.md`

## Rule Card I.14 — Downfall / Calamity Checks The Second Place And Asset Lots

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.29.1-41 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 112-116]` `[new to Dorotheus]`

Use when:

- a wealth/status reading has strong negative testimony requiring source-backed constraint.

Required data:

- second place and ruler;
- Lot of assets and ruler;
- Lot of livelihood if formula verified;
- malefic placement in angles or asset places;
- Moon condition;
- benefic mitigation.

Procedure:

1. Check second place and its lord before making asset claims.
2. Use Lot of assets as a derived asset anchor only with formula/source recorded.
3. Treat malefics in asset-relevant stakes or Lot positions as pressure.
4. Check benefic testimony before escalating severity.
5. Keep the judgment tied to assets/status, not general character.

Guardrail:

Avoid fatalistic language. Translate "downfall" into constrained asset/status testimony unless a historical analysis requires stronger wording.

Destination:

- `rules/lots-and-fortune.md`
- `rules/delineation-output-rules.md`

- source text invokes male/female, eastern/western, diurnal/nocturnal, or directional sign categories.

Required data:

- sign categories;
- triplicity rulers by day/night;
- topic context.

Procedure:

1. Record sign category only when the method calls for it.
2. Use category as a condition modifier.
3. Do not replace domicile/triplicity/ruler logic with sign-category symbolism.

Guardrail:

Avoid modern essentializing claims from ancient gendered sign categories.
