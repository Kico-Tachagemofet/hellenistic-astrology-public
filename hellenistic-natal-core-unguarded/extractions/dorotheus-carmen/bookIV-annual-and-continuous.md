# Dorotheus Extraction — Book IV Annual And Continuous Timing

Source: Dorotheus of Sidon, *Carmen Astrologicum: The 'Umar al-Tabari Translation*, trans. Benjamin Dykes, 2017, Book IV, printed pp. 197-229.

Transmission note: source is Dorotheus through 'Umar al-Tabari, translated by Dykes. Dykes flags several textual ambiguities in Book IV, especially around annual revolution, monthly lords, daily lords, and transit passages.

Brennan cross-ref: Ch.17 annual profections, Ch.9 configurations, Ch.14 benefic/malefic testimony. Dorotheus Book IV does not directly confirm Brennan Ch.18 Zodiacal Releasing.

## Method Takeaway

Book IV strongly validates annual profections as a whole-sign timing filter and adds solar revolution/transit layers not yet present in v1:

```text
profected sign -> lord of year -> natal root condition -> annual/revolution condition -> activated natal planets -> transit repetition
```

Do not integrate Book IV medical/death material as client-facing prediction. Its reusable value is timing architecture and condition logic.

## Rule Card IV.1 — Annual Profection Counts One Sign Per Year From Ascendant

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* IV.1.1-3 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed p. 197]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`

Use when:

- identifying the activated annual place.

Required data:

- Ascendant sign;
- age/year being judged;
- whole sign houses;
- domicile ruler of activated sign.

Procedure:

1. Begin from the Ascendant sign at birth.
2. Count one sign for each year until reaching the target year.
3. Identify the sign reached as the sign of the year.
4. Identify the lord of that sign as lord of the year.

Guardrail:

Use whole signs for this profection count.

Destination:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

## Rule Card IV.2 — Lord Of Year Requires Natal Root Audit

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* IV.1.3-8 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 197-198]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`

Use when:

- judging quality of an annual profection year.

Required data:

- lord of year;
- lord's natal place and condition;
- phase/visibility;
- under-rays and retrograde status;
- dignity/own or alien place;
- luminary testimony if available.

Procedure:

1. Audit the lord of year in the root nativity.
2. Check whether it was eastern/western, under rays, retrograde, or increasing/decreasing.
3. Check whether it is in its own place or an alien/enemy place.
4. Use this condition to constrain annual judgment.

Guardrail:

A year is not judged by activated place alone. The lord's natal condition is mandatory.

Destination:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`
- `rules/planet-condition-audit.md`

## Rule Card IV.6 — Years Reaching Natal Planet Signs Activate Natal Tenants

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* IV.1.21-34 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 200-202]` `[cross-ref: Brennan Ch.17]`

Use when:

- a profected year reaches a sign occupied by a natal planet.

Required data:

- natal planets by sign;
- activated profected sign;
- whether the same planet currently occupies or aspects that sign in the revolution/transit;
- benefic/malefic witnesses.

Procedure:

1. Identify natal planets in the profected sign.
2. Treat those planets as activated tenants.
3. Check whether the same planet or another planet currently occupies/aspects the sign.
4. Apply benefic mitigation or malefic intensification.
5. Preserve the natal promise: activation does not invent a topic from nothing.

Guardrail:

Do not ignore activated tenants in favor of the lord of year alone.

Destination:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md`

## Rule Card IV.13 — Transit Arrival Quality Depends On Natal Aspect Relation

Source: `[Dorotheus *Carmen* IV.4.1-8 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017, printed pp. 220-222]` `[new to Dorotheus]` `[needs verification]`

Use when:

- comparing a planet's current transit to signs it related to natally.

Required data:

- natal planet sign;
- signs the planet opposed, squared, and trined natally;
- current transit sign of that planet;
- benefic/malefic identity and sect/condition.

Procedure:

1. Identify the sign currently reached by the transiting planet.
2. Ask how that sign related to the planet's natal position.
3. Treat transit to a sign opposed or overcome by the natal planet as harsher.
4. Treat transit to a trine of the natal position as easier, especially for malefics.
5. Separate this from generic transits to natal planets.

Guardrail:

Dykes flags this section as mangled. Do not implement until compared with Schmidt/Excerpts and other sources.

Destination:

- `rules/timing-techniques.md` future transit filter
- `rules/aspect-reception-rules.md` future comparison
