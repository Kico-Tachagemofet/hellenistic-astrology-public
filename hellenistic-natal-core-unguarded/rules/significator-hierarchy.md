### Divinatory / Spiritual Aptitude — Focus Notes

This row is distinct from `Spirituality / religion` above: that row reads the
chart's relation to god/temple/doctrine (the 9th-place topic); this row reads
the native's capacity to receive, translate, and act on signs / dreams /
oracular contact (a stacked aptitude through 9th + 12th, supported by the
luminary of sect, perception/messenger planets, and Daimon).

`[Brennan Ch.10]`, printed pp. 340-363, anchors the 9th (god, divination, Sun
joy, dreams in some treatments) and the 12th (Saturn joy, hidden/isolating,
contact with deep or contrary forces). The pairing 9th + 12th and the
supporting witness list below are the project's v2.1 synthesis layer; the
underlying material in each source is cited individually.

#### Anchor and topic significator rationale

- **9th place (anchor 1)** — sacred / divinatory / oracular / philosophical /
  astrological / dream / prophetic material clusters here.
  - `[Brennan Ch.10]`, printed pp. 340-363 (9th = god, religion, divination,
    Sun joy).
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II.5, II.7-8]` (Mark Riley trans.) — IX as God; III as
    Goddess; temple, prophecy, divination, dream, sacred office material
    repeatedly routed through 9th.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* III]` Bram printed (9th-house planets repeatedly
    trigger sacred / temple / divinatory / philosophical / astrological /
    dream / magical-role material — extract role and aptitude only, exclude
    operational magical procedure per v2.1 scope).
  - `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I.9 via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` — 9th-house
    chapter for travel + divinatory / religious significations.

- **12th place (anchor 2)** — hidden / nocturnal / contact-with-deep-or-
  contrary-forces field; Saturn's joy.
  - `[Brennan Ch.10]`, printed pp. 340-363 (12th = bad spirit, hidden,
    isolation, Saturn joy).
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* III]` Bram printed (6th/12th material can become
    active when 10th/MC is unsupported; expression hidden / servile /
    obstructed — relevant for nocturnal-channel aptitude rather than overt
    public action).
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II]` confirms 12th-as-hidden / contrary doctrine.

The pairing 9th + 12th as a combined divinatory/spiritual aptitude anchor is
`[project heuristic]`: 9th gives overt divinatory channel; 12th gives
nocturnal / hidden / Saturn-joy depth. Together they form a stacked aptitude
field rather than a single topic.

#### Supporting witness rationale

- **Moon** — natural significator of perception, dreams, body's receptive
  modality.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` Moon's traditional significations + Ch.10 3rd-place
    Moon-joy (goddess / oracular Moon material).
  - `[Valens *Anth.* I]` Moon as perception, dream, oracular receiver.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* IV.1-16]` Moon phase / application / separation as
    central audit field for divinatory / contact-readiness.

- **Mercury** — messenger / sign-reader / translator of symbols.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* I, II]` repeatedly links Mercury with prophecy, dream
    interpretation, sacred letters, divination, astrological practice.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* III]` Mercury common in Thema Mundi argument
    (morning-rising with Sun by day, evening-rising with Moon by night) —
    structural position lets Mercury translate between the luminaries; 9th-
    place Mercury repeatedly triggers astrological/divinatory role material.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` Mercury baseline + Ch.10 1st-house Mercury joy.

- **Saturn by sect** — depth, stillness, initiation, capacity to bear
  prolonged contact with hidden or contrary forces.
  - `[Brennan Ch.10]` Saturn joy in 12th (pp. 336-340).
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* III]` Saturn material consistently links to depth,
    burden, isolation; in 12th this routes to long endurance with hidden
    matter rather than to surface action.
  - **By sect**: diurnal Saturn (of-sect) is the supportive form for sustained
    depth-work and ordeal-tolerance; nocturnal Saturn (contrary-to-sect)
    weighs as obstruction / overload rather than stable depth-capacity. Per
    `planet-condition-audit.md` sect rules.

- **sect light's relation to 9th and 12th** — whether the native's primary
  luminary configures or averts from the divinatory anchors.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` sect light doctrine + `[Brennan Ch.10]` configuration
    to the divinatory places.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II]` time-lord initialization starts with sect light
    (Sun in day, Moon in night); divinatory aptitude routes through
    whichever luminary "owns" the chart and whether it sees 9/12.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* IX]` (Fortune-Daimon-Ascendant methods) repeatedly uses
    sect light's relation to derived anchors as a primary witness.

- **Spirit (Daimon)** — the chart's mental/intentional/volitional point;
  primary Lot for action, choice, and (for this focus) the direction in
  which divinatory / spiritual intention can be exercised. Formula already
  locked in `lots-and-fortune.md` per `[Brennan Ch.16]`, pp. 521-522, 525,
  with cross-ref to `[Valens *Anth.* IX]` and `[Firmicus *Math.* IV.18]`.
  (Firmicus Bram printed Daemon=Fortune appearance is `needs verification`;
  see `cross-source-method-comparison.md`.)

#### Lots layer (notes only; main table keeps the v1 format)

- **Spirit (Daimon)** — already in v1; primary Lot for this focus. ≥2 source
  support (Brennan Ch.16 + Valens *Anth.* IX); Firmicus Bram printed has
  formula ambiguity logged in cross-source layer.
- **Fortune** — relevant when bodily / circumstantial channel of contact
  matters (e.g., physiological onset of perception). Already in v1.
- **Lot of Religion** — `[Source-only candidate, needs verification]`.
  Dorotheus Bk.I.9 (via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017) is the primary
  attestation; other sources do not confirm a single distinct formula. **Do
  not include as a default Lot.** See `cross-source-method-comparison.md`
  for formula variants and v2.x defer notes; `lots-and-fortune.md` registers
  it as candidate only.
- **Lot of Eros** — optional / topic-specific. `[Brennan Ch.16]` Hermetic
  Lots survey covers Eros; Dorotheus topic Lots mention friendship/Eros.
  Use only when the question explicitly involves erotic / affective
  divinatory channel; not a default supporting Lot for general divinatory
  aptitude.

#### Discipline reminders for this focus

- This row is a **layer map**, not a reading cookbook. Do not turn it into
  "9th-house Moon means X" placement readings; route through the full
  `planet-condition-audit.md` per usual.
- For each witness above, **provenance is for that specific witness only**.
  The `[Brennan Ch.10]` anchor citation does not transfer to the Mercury /
  Saturn / Daimon detail lines.
- **`needs verification` items stay explicit**: Lot of Religion is candidate
  only; not promoted to the main supporting-witness list.
- v2.1 scope guard: read aptitude / form only. Do not extract operational
  magical procedure from Firmicus / Valens (Picatrix-Agrippa-class
  procedure remains v3+ deferred per `v2-roadmap.md`).
- This focus does not auto-activate Fortune/Spirit as a data gate. Per the
  general focus-row Note above, `reading-workflow.md` records Lots module
  status (`run`/`skip`/`blocked`) when this focus is selected.

### Force / Power Signature — Focus Notes

This row is distinct from `Whole life / character` above: that row asks
"who is this person" (Asc + Asc ruler + sect light + Lots + angles read as
a person-shape); this row asks "which planetary archetype dominates this
chart / carries this person's force-modality" — the procedure foregrounds
Lord-of-the-geniture computation and archetypal cluster identification
rather than person-as-actor synthesis.

`[Brennan Ch.12-13]`, printed pp. 426-484, frames significator + ruler
chain + Lord-of-the-geniture as a tradition layer. `[Valens *Anth.* II.2-3]`
(Mark Riley trans.), `[Firmicus *Math.* II.27-29]` (Bram 1975 trans.), and
`[Dorotheus *Carmen* I via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` supply parallel
planet-as-signator / combined-condition material. The five-vote procedure
below is the project's v2.X synthesis layer; the underlying material in
each source is cited individually.

#### Anchor and topic significator rationale

- **Lord of the geniture (primary anchor)** — the planet whose combined
  rulership + condition weight makes it the chart's archetypal lord. **Not**
  identical to "ruler of the Ascendant" and **not** identical to "most
  exalted planet by single dignity"; the integrated lord across rulership +
  condition + sect + angularity + luminary configuration.
  - `[Brennan Ch.12-13]`, printed pp. 426-484 — significators, ruler chain,
    house-ruler integration, Lord-of-the-geniture frame.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II.2-3]` (Mark Riley trans.) — planet-as-signator
    survey + house-by-house ruler weighing.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II.27-29]` (Bram 1975 trans.) — combined dignity /
    sect / angularity / aspect judgment for planet efficacy.
  - `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` — planet by
    sign / dignity material treating planet quality as character signator.

- **Sect light + most dignified of sect (topic significator)** — the
  sect-luminary itself + whichever in-sect planet emerges from the audit
  with the cleanest condition.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` sect-light doctrine + `[Brennan Ch.8]` essential
    dignities table.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* I, II]` in-sect / contrary-to-sect weighing.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II.7]` sect attributions; `[Firmicus *Math.* II.27-29]`
    sect + dignity combined audit.
  - `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` planet-by-
    sign material implicitly differentiated by sect.

#### Supporting witness rationale

- **Chart ruler (Asc ruler)** — distinct axis from Lord-of-the-geniture;
  the ruler of the rising sign is one input to the Lord computation but
  does not pre-empt the integrated weighing. Same-Planet Multi-Role
  Handling applies when Asc ruler coincides with Lord-of-the-geniture
  (i.e., it deepens a single line of evidence rather than doubling
  testimony).
  - `[Brennan Ch.12-13]` chart-ruler vs Lord-of-the-geniture distinction.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II.27]` chart ruler treatment (Firmicus's "most-
    rulership-of-the-Moon-and-Asc" approach noted in
    `cross-source-method-comparison.md` Flag 3, not adopted into v1).

- **Mars / Saturn / Venus archetypal weight** — these three carry the most
  distinct "force-modality" archetypes (Mars = compulsion / cut, Saturn =
  constraint / depth / endurance, Venus = drawing / pull / attraction);
  their relative audit weights characterize the chart's force signature
  even when none is the Lord-of-the-geniture.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` planet natures.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* I]` planet-by-planet quality material.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II.7]` sect attributions + planet character.
  - `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` planet-as-
    character signator across signs.

- **Cluster / stellium patterns** — **≥3 planets in the same sign or
  whole-sign place** count as a cluster and shift the signature toward
  that sign's lord / archetype. Sub-3-planet groupings are co-presence,
  not cluster.
  - `[Brennan Ch.9]` configurations + co-presence material.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II]` multi-planet place treatments.
  - `[project heuristic]` for the ≥3 floor — prevents inflated "stellium"
    labeling and routes 2-planet groupings through co-presence rules in
    `aspect-reception-rules.md` instead.

- **Overcoming network** — who dominates whom via superior square /
  overcoming. The superior-position planet inflects the under-position
  planet's expression toward the superior's modality; cumulative
  overcoming patterns build the chart's force-direction map.
  - `[Brennan Ch.9]`, printed pp. 296-324, dexterity + overcoming
    doctrine.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II]` dexter-sinister aspect material.
  - Already encoded in `aspect-reception-rules.md`; this focus uses the
    overcoming map as a signature-shaping input rather than re-deriving it.

#### Lord-of-the-geniture procedure (`[project heuristic]` at assembly level; individual votes sourced)

1. **Identify essential rulers for the chart's most significant places** —
   Asc, sect-light's place, Lot of Spirit, MC. For each, record: domicile
   ruler, exaltation ruler (if any), triplicity ruler (by sect), bound
   ruler, decan ruler. Each is one vote. Use `dignities-reference.md` for
   canonical rulership data.
2. **Tally** votes across the above. Planets accumulating votes across
   multiple places are candidates.
3. **Apply condition modifiers** — for each vote-bearing candidate, run
   `planet-condition-audit.md` and weight:
   - of-sect ✓ / contrary-to-sect ✗
   - angular ✓ / succedent neutral / cadent ✗
   - configured to sect light ✓ / averse to sect light ✗
   - visibility / combustion condition (per planet's visibility profile)
4. **Output 3-5 candidates** with vote count + condition summary + final
   Lord (the planet whose combined position is strongest). When candidates
   tie, output **mixed-Lord** with both stated explicitly (no averaging).
5. **Confidence**: Moderate default; Speculative if 3-vote spread; mixed
   if condition severely splits top candidates.

This is **not** the Arabic-tradition **Almuten** algorithm (5/4/3/2/1
weighted by domicile/exaltation/triplicity/bound/decan, then summed). The
Almuten is post-Hellenistic and out of scope. The procedure above keeps
rulership-tier votes equal-weight and lets condition do the weighting,
which fits the Hellenistic sources cited.

#### Discipline reminders for this focus

- **Not a placement cookbook**. "Mars in Leo means power" is exactly the
  cookbook output this row is designed to avoid; signature weight emerges
  only after the full audit, never from raw sign / place placement.
- **Audit-first gate**: do not name a Lord-of-the-geniture or a dominant
  archetype before completing `planet-condition-audit.md` for the
  candidate planets. The five-vote tally is necessary but not sufficient
  — condition decides whether votes translate into actual signature
  weight (echoes `v2.x-case-05-rule-extension-candidates-plan.md` §A
  Pattern 3 closure: rulership and condition are independent axes
  combined at reading time).
- **Cluster floor**: ≥3 planets co-present is the minimum for "cluster";
  2 planets co-present is co-presence-only.
- **Same-Planet Multi-Role**: when Asc ruler ≡ Lord-of-the-geniture ≡
  topic ruler (or any subset), follow `Same-Planet Multi-Role Handling`
  below — repeated role deepens a single testimony line rather than
  producing independent second testimony.
- **Distinction from `Whole life / character`**: that row reads the chart
  as a person-shape; this row reads the chart as a planetary-archetype-
  weight map. Both can run on the same chart and produce overlapping but
  non-identical findings; the v2.X Phase B case (case-06a) is the first
  test of distinctness — if force-signature reading does not produce
  findings distinct from a character reading, the focus row is a
  candidate for downgrade to a character-focus sub-section.
- **Provenance per witness**: source citations attach to the specific
  witness line and are not transferable across.
- This focus does not auto-activate Fortune/Spirit as a data gate. Per
  the general focus-row Note above, `reading-workflow.md` records Lots
  module status (`run`/`skip`/`blocked`) when this focus is selected.

### 父母 / 家庭关系 — Focus Notes

This row is distinct from `Whole life / character` above: that row reads
the native as a person-shape (Asc + Asc ruler + sect light + Lots + angles);
this row reads the **family-of-origin field** — the parental images carried
in the chart, the home/foundations stratum, and the way elders / lineage
weight presses on the native's footing. There is no prior v1 row that
anchors parents/home, so this focus fills a gap rather than re-cutting an
existing one.

`[Brennan Ch.10]`, printed pp. 340-363, anchors the 4th (home, parents,
foundations, property, endings) and the 10th (action, reputation, and — in
some doctrines — a second parental reading). `[Valens *Anth.* II]` (Mark
Riley trans.) and `[Firmicus *Math.* II.19]` (Bram 1975 trans.) carry
parallel place-topic material with internal variance over which house
reads father vs mother (see `cross-source-method-comparison.md` Flag 14
for the cross-source register). The pairing 4 + 10 as a combined parental
field and the supporting witness list below are the project's v2.2
synthesis layer; the underlying material in each source is cited
individually.

#### Anchor and topic significator rationale

- **4th place (anchor 1)** — home, parents-base, foundations, property,
  endings; the family-of-origin stratum in whole-sign tradition.
  - `[Brennan Ch.10]`, printed pp. 340-363 (4th = parents, home, property,
    foundations, endings).
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II.5, II.7-8]` (Mark Riley trans.) — IV repeatedly
    routes parent material, household, land, ancestors, paternal heritage.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II.19]` Bram printed — 4th-house material includes
    parents and patrimony; consistent with Valens / Brennan baseline.
  - `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` — 4th-house
    chapter for parents / property.

- **10th place (anchor 2)** — action and reputation primarily, but in
  parallel doctrines also a **second parental reading** (often father in
  Hellenistic texts that split paternal reading away from the 4th).
  - `[Brennan Ch.10]`, printed pp. 340-363 (10th = action, reputation;
    secondary parental reading noted).
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II]` — 10th material includes occupation but Bk.II
    also routes a parental reading through 10th in selected chapters.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II.19]` Bram printed — 10th-house material in
    Firmicus's place-topic survey includes a paternal channel parallel to
    the 4th reading.

The pairing 4 + 10 as a combined parental anchor is `[project heuristic]`:
4 gives the home/foundations/ancestral base, 10 gives the second-parent /
public-lineage axis. Together they form a stacked family-of-origin field
rather than committing the rule to a single "4 = mother, 10 = father" or
"4 = father" split — which doctrine to apply is read off natural
significators (Sun / Moon) and the audit, not pre-decided by the focus
row. See `cross-source-method-comparison.md` Flag 14 for source variance.

- **4th ruler + 10th ruler (topic significators)** — the rulers carry
  the parental topic into wherever they land; condition + destination
  determine how the family field is enacted vs how the public-lineage
  axis presents.
  - `[Brennan Ch.12-13]`, printed pp. 426-484 — topic-ruler-flow
    formula; rulers as carriers of place topics.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II]` — house-by-house ruler weighing, including
    parental places.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* IV.19-20]` Bram printed — ruler-destination
    treatment as topic carrier.
  - Procedure already encoded in `house-topic-framework.md` (Source
    house S → ruler R → destination D); this focus uses that formula
    twice (once for 4, once for 10) rather than re-deriving it.

#### Supporting witness rationale

- **Sun (natural significator of father)** — paternal archetype in
  Hellenistic doctrine; condition of Sun characterizes the father
  image and the paternal channel of inheritance.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` Sun's traditional significations including
    father / authority / fame.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* I]` Sun-as-father in planet-quality material.
  - `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` Sun
    as paternal natural significator in topic chapters.

- **Moon (natural significator of mother)** — maternal archetype;
  condition of Moon characterizes the mother image, the body-of-origin
  bond, and the receptive / nutritive parental channel.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` Moon's traditional significations including
    mother / body / nurture.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* I]` Moon-as-mother in planet-quality material.
  - `[Dorotheus *Carmen* I via 'Umar al-Tabari → Dykes 2017]` Moon as
    maternal natural significator in topic chapters.

- **Saturn (elders / ancestors / lineage weight)** — Saturn signifies
  elder figures, grandparents, ancestral weight, and the lineage-
  pressure / inheritance-of-burden axis of the family field.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` Saturn baseline (elder / age / endurance / depth).
  - `[Valens *Anth.* I]` Saturn-as-elder / paternal-elder in selected
    chapters; ancestor and burden material.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* III]` Bram printed — Saturn material routes
    elder, burden, ancestral pressure, and isolation themes.

- **House tenants (planets in 4 and/or 10)** — local actors inside the
  parental field; tenants color the place expression independently of
  the ruler's destination and must be audited per place.
  - `[Brennan Ch.13]`, printed pp. 440-460 — tenants as evidence
    distinct from rulers.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* II]` multi-planet place treatments routinely
    treat tenants as primary local actors.
  - Encoded in `house-topic-framework.md` Evidence Types row "Planets
    in topic house = local tenants / actors inside that topic field";
    this focus uses that distinction rather than re-deriving it.

- **Sect light's family channel** — whether the chart's primary
  luminary (Sun by day, Moon by night) configures the 4th or 10th
  determines which parental channel is the native's "owned" one and
  which is the structurally averted one.
  - `[Brennan Ch.7]` sect light doctrine + `[Brennan Ch.10]`
    configuration to topic places.
  - `[Firmicus *Math.* II]` time-lord initialization via sect light;
    sect-light configuration to topic places weighted as primary witness.
  - `[Valens *Anth.* IX]` (Fortune-Daimon-Ascendant methods)
    consistently treats sect light's relation to derived anchors as
    primary witness.

#### Discipline reminders for this focus

- **Not a placement cookbook**. "Sun in 4th means dominant father" is
  exactly the cookbook output this row is designed to avoid; the
  parental field reads emerge only after the full audit of Sun + Moon +
  Saturn + 4th-ruler + 10th-ruler condition, never from raw sign /
  place placement. **Lens reframing ≠ placement cookbook**: when a
  witness changes how a planet's force is read (e.g., Saturn read as
  ancestral-burden under the family lens vs depth-capacity under the
  divinatory lens), the lens reframes the *modality* the audit-derived
  condition is applied to, not the audit verdict itself.
- **Audit-first gate**: do not name a parental image or a lineage
  pattern before completing `planet-condition-audit.md` for Sun, Moon,
  Saturn, 4th-ruler, and 10th-ruler. The natural-significator slot is
  necessary but not sufficient — condition decides whether Sun's
  paternal significance reads as bright-and-visible vs combust-and-
  obscured (and parallel for Moon-as-mother).
- **≥2-source triangulation gate**: each witness above carries its
  own provenance line; the `[Brennan Ch.10]` anchor citation does
  **not** transfer to the Sun / Moon / Saturn / sect-light detail
  lines. Single-source claims (e.g., a doctrine attested only in
  Firmicus's 10th-as-father variant) must be flagged
  `[Source-only candidate]` and treated speculatively.
- **4 vs 10 doctrine variance is preserved, not collapsed**: the focus
  row does not commit to a single "4 = mother, 10 = father" or
  "4 = both parents" split. Source-internal variance is registered in
  `cross-source-method-comparison.md` Flag 14; the reading uses
  natural significators (Sun / Moon) + sect-light channel to determine
  which parent reads through which place in the specific chart.
- **Same-Planet Multi-Role applies**: if Sun ≡ 4th ruler, or Saturn
  ≡ 10th ruler, or Moon ≡ tenant of 4th, follow the
  `Same-Planet Multi-Role Handling` block below — repeated role
  deepens a single testimony line rather than producing independent
  second testimony.
- **Distinction from `Whole life / character`**: that row reads the
  chart as a person-shape; this row reads the chart as a parental-
  field / family-of-origin map. Both can run on the same chart and
  produce overlapping but non-identical findings.
- **Stage 2 topic-module pointer**: this focus row only stands up the
  hierarchy layer. The modern application of this focus — running a
  per-chart family reading as a session output — is the `family`
  topic-module spec in `rules/topic-modules.md` §4.1.3 (composite of
  4 + 10). The focus row is the rule layer; the topic module is the
  Stage 2 session shape.
- This focus does not auto-activate Fortune/Spirit as a data gate. Per
  the general focus-row Note above, `reading-workflow.md` records Lots
  module status (`run`/`skip`/`blocked`) when this focus is selected.

### 学业 / 学习能力 — Focus Notes

This row has no prior counterpart in the v1 focus rows: `Whole life /
character` covers life-as-shape, `Spirituality / religion` covers the
9th-place sacred axis, and `Divinatory / spiritual aptitude` covers
the 9 + 12 oracular-aptitude stack — none of them reads **学习 capacity
as such**: how the native takes in / digests / structures / extends
knowledge. This row fills that gap by anchoring on Mercury as the
mind / learning natural significator, with the lower-mind (3rd) and
higher-mind (9th) places as the topic anchors.

`[Brennan Ch.7]`, printed pp. 200-208, anchors Mercury as mind / messenger
/ learning / sign-translation. `[Brennan Ch.10]`, printed pp. 340-363,
anchors the 3rd (siblings, lower mind, short trips, local instruction,
Moon joy / goddess) and the 9th (god, philosophy, long study, higher
mind, Sun joy). `[Valens *Anth.* II]` (Mark Riley trans.) and
`[Firmicus *Math.* II.19]` (Bram 1975 trans.) supply parallel place-
topic material. The composite Mercury + 3 + 9 + sect-light-to-Mercury
+ Saturn / Jupiter / Venus modifier set is the project's v2.2 synthesis
layer; the underlying material in each source is cited individually.

#### Discipline reminders for this focus

- **Not a placement cookbook**. "Mercury in 9th means scholar" is
  exactly the cookbook output this row is designed to avoid; learning-
  capacity reads emerge only after the full audit of Mercury + 3rd-
  ruler + 9th-ruler + sect-light + Saturn/Jupiter/Venus modifiers,
  never from raw sign / place placement. **Lens reframing ≠ placement
  cookbook**: when Mercury is read under the learning lens vs the
  divinatory lens (Mercury appears as supporting witness in
  `Divinatory / spiritual aptitude` Focus Notes above), the lens
  reframes the *modality* the audit-derived condition applies to —
  one reading focuses on Mercury-as-mind-instrument; the other
  focuses on Mercury-as-symbol-translator. Audit verdict carries
  across; lens picks which face of that verdict is foregrounded.
- **Audit-first gate**: do not name a learning style, suitable
  subjects, or scholarship-capacity verdict before completing
  `planet-condition-audit.md` for Mercury, 3rd-ruler, 9th-ruler, and
  the Saturn / Jupiter / Venus modifiers being foregrounded.
- **≥2-source triangulation gate**: each witness above carries its
  own provenance line; single-source claims (e.g., a Mercury-
  reception nuance attested only in one source) must be flagged
  `[Source-only candidate]` and treated speculatively.
- **Mercury sect undeclared cases**: when Mercury falls within the
  under-rays band and the visibility-direction test cannot resolve
  sect leaning, the sect-light-to-Mercury witness reads as
  `undeclared` — do not force a verdict in either direction. Per
  `planet-condition-audit.md` Mercury sect rule + `cross-source-
  method-comparison.md` Flag 1.
- **Saturn / Jupiter / Venus modifier provenance is per-modifier**:
  the `[Brennan Ch.7]` baseline citation does **not** mean "all three
  modifiers attest in every other source"; Valens / Firmicus / Dorotheus
  citations are tied to each specific modifier in the witness rationale.
- **Same-Planet Multi-Role applies**: if Mercury ≡ 3rd ruler, or
  Mercury ≡ 9th ruler, or Jupiter ≡ 9th ruler, follow the
  `Same-Planet Multi-Role Handling` block below — repeated role
  deepens a single testimony line rather than producing independent
  second testimony.
- **Distinction from `Spirituality / religion` and `Divinatory /
  spiritual aptitude`**: those two read sacred / oracular axes; this
  row reads cognitive / educational capacity. Mercury and the 9th
  appear in all three rows under different lenses — overlap is
  expected; collapsing the rows is not.
- **Stage 2 topic-module pointer**: this focus row only stands up the
  hierarchy layer. The modern application — running a per-chart
  学业 / learning reading as a session output — is the `learning`
  topic-module spec in `rules/topic-modules.md` §4.1.3 (composite of
  Mercury + 3 + 9 + sect-light-to-Mercury). The focus row is the rule

## Step 2: Assign Roles

```text
Primary significator: the actor or body of the matter.
Topic significator: the house ruler or Lot ruler carrying the topic.
Natural significator: planet naturally related to the topic.
Witnesses: aspects, rulers, Lots, time lords, angular planets.
```

## Same-Planet Multi-Role Handling

`[Brennan Ch.13]`, printed pp. 440-460, supports listing ruler chains separately and tracking when one planet rules more than one topic house; `[Brennan Ch.16]`, printed p. 517, supports judging a Lot through its ruler. The evidence-counting rules below are `[project heuristic]`.

When one planet carries multiple roles:

1. List all roles in the roster and in that planet's audit block: topic ruler, Lot ruler, natural significator, tenant, time lord, house ruler, or witness.
2. When the same planet occupies multiple significator roles in the current focus, for example Lot ruler + topic ruler, Asc ruler + topic ruler, Asc ruler + Lord of Year, topic ruler + Lord of Year, or natural significator + topic ruler, the repeated identity does not produce independent testimonies. Treat the repeated role as a repeated or deepened line of evidence. Do not count it as an independent second testimony for the Evidence Standard's `2+ independent testimonies` threshold unless it adds a distinct ruler, distinct configured witness, or distinct derived-place testimony.
3. If the same planet is ruler `R` in more than one topic chain, run the audit once in each chain context, then merge the repeated condition evidence in the final report. Keep the source houses and flows separate unless another rule joins them.
4. If a planet's multi-role load creates a bottleneck, report the bottleneck explicitly instead of inflating confidence through repeated mentions of the same planet.

## Step 3: Audit Before Interpreting

Do not interpret a significator until `planet-condition-audit.md` has been applied.

Minimum audit:

- sect relationship;
- essential dignity;
- house placement and angularity;
- visibility/combustion condition;
- aspects and reception from benefics/malefics;
- applying/separating relationships when the question is event-like.

## Evidence Standard

A strong conclusion usually needs at least two of the following:

- topic house condition;
- topic ruler condition;
- natural significator condition;
- relevant Lot or time-lord activation;
- repeated testimony from aspects/reception.

A single symbol can be reported as a clue, not a verdict.
